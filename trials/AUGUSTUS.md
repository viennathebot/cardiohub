# AUGUSTUS

**Antithrombotic Therapy after Acute Coronary Syndrome or PCI in Atrial Fibrillation**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2019 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1817083 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT02415400 |
| **Bottom Line** | Apixaban + P2Y12 inhibitor (without aspirin) reduces bleeding vs warfarin + DAPT in AF + ACS/PCI |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | 2×2 factorial randomized trial, open-label |
| **N** | 4,614 |
| **Centers** | 492 sites, 33 countries |
| **Population** | AF + recent ACS and/or PCI requiring anticoagulation |
| **Mean Age** | 71 years |
| **Mean CHA₂DS₂-VASc** | 3.9 |
| **Randomization** | 2×2 factorial: Apixaban vs Warfarin AND Aspirin vs No Aspirin |

### The Clinical Problem
- AF patients need anticoagulation
- ACS/PCI patients need antiplatelet therapy
- Combining all three ("triple therapy") = high bleeding risk

### The Question
What's the optimal antithrombotic regimen after ACS/PCI in patients with AF?

### Arms (2×2 Factorial)

| Factor 1 | Factor 2 | Regimen |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Apixaban | No aspirin | Apixaban + P2Y12 only |
| Apixaban | Aspirin | Apixaban + P2Y12 + ASA |
| Warfarin | No aspirin | Warfarin + P2Y12 only |
| Warfarin | Aspirin | Warfarin + P2Y12 + ASA |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Major or clinically relevant non-major (CRNM) bleeding at 6 months** (ISTH criteria)

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- Death or hospitalization (composite)
- Death, stroke, MI, stent thrombosis, urgent revascularization (ischemic composite)
- Individual components

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint: Bleeding (6 Months)

#### Apixaban vs Warfarin

| Outcome | Apixaban | Warfarin | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|----------|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Major/CRNM bleeding** | **10.5%** | **14.7%** | **0.69 (0.58-0.81)** | **<0.001** |

**ARR = 4.2%** | **NNT = 24**

#### Aspirin vs No Aspirin

| Outcome | Aspirin | No Aspirin | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|---------|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Major/CRNM bleeding** | **16.1%** | **9.0%** | **1.89 (1.59-2.24)** | **<0.001** |

**ARI = 7.1%** | **NNH = 14**

### The Four Regimens Compared

| Regimen | Bleeding Rate |
|---------|---------------|
| Apixaban + P2Y12 only | **7.3%** |
| Apixaban + P2Y12 + ASA | 13.8% |
| Warfarin + P2Y12 only | 10.9% |
| Warfarin + P2Y12 + ASA | 18.7% |

**Apixaban + P2Y12 without aspirin = lowest bleeding**

| Outcome | Aspirin | No Aspirin | HR | P-value |
|---------|---------|------------|-----|---------|
| Death or hospitalization | 26.2% | 24.7% | NS | NS |

## Key Findings Summary

| Question | Answer | Evidence |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Apixaban vs Warfarin? | **Apixaban better** | 31% less bleeding, fewer hospitalizations |
| Aspirin vs No Aspirin? | **Drop aspirin** | 47% less bleeding, no ischemic penalty |
| Best regimen? | **Apixaban + P2Y12 alone** | 7.3% bleeding (lowest) |

## Contemporary Context: Triple Therapy Trials

### The Evolution

| Trial | Year | N | Finding |
|-------|------|---|---------|
| WOEST | 2013 | 573 | First to show dual OK |
| RE-DUAL PCI | 2017 | 2,725 | Dabigatran dual ≈ warfarin triple |
| PIONEER AF-PCI | 2016 | 2,124 | Rivaroxaban dual better |
| **AUGUSTUS** | 2019 | 4,614 | Apixaban + P2Y12, no ASA best |
| ENTRUST-AF PCI | 2019 | 1,506 | Edoxaban dual ≈ triple |

### AUGUSTUS's Unique Contribution
- **Factorial design** — separated DOAC vs warfarin AND aspirin vs no aspirin
- **Largest trial** — N=4,614
- **Both ACS and PCI** — not just elective PCI

## Practical Application

### Current Recommendations (Post-AUGUSTUS Era)

| Phase | Duration | Regimen |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Peri-PCI | 0-7 days | Triple therapy (brief) |
| Early | 1-4 weeks | Dual (DOAC + P2Y12) |
| Long-term | After 4-12 weeks | OAC alone (drop P2Y12) |

### Dropping Aspirin

| Scenario | When to Drop ASA |
|----------|------------------|
| Low ischemic risk | Immediately after PCI |
| Moderate risk | 1-7 days post-PCI |
| High ischemic risk | 1-4 weeks post-PCI |

### DOAC Choice

| DOAC | Evidence |
|------|----------|
| **Apixaban** | AUGUSTUS (strongest data) |
| Rivaroxaban | PIONEER AF-PCI |
| Dabigatran | RE-DUAL PCI |
| Edoxaban | ENTRUST-AF PCI |

**Apixaban has most robust evidence**

---

## Guideline Integration

### 2023 ACC/AHA AF Guidelines
- Dual therapy (DOAC + P2Y12) preferred over triple
- Aspirin discontinued early (1-7 days if low-risk)
- DOACs preferred over warfarin
- Duration of dual therapy: 1-12 months based on risk

### ESC 2020 AF + ACS/PCI
- Triple therapy: short as possible (up to 1 week)
- Dual therapy: up to 12 months
- Then OAC monotherapy

---

## Limitations

1. **Open-label** — no blinding (INR monitoring unblinded warfarin)
2. **Not powered for ischemic events** — designed for bleeding
3. **6-month follow-up** — short term
4. **Clopidogrel mostly** — 92% on clopidogrel (not ticagrelor/prasugrel)
5. **No true warfarin triple control** — factorial design complicates

---

## Controversies

### Is There an Ischemic Signal?
- MI: 3.9% (aspirin) vs 3.0% (no aspirin) — NS but trending
- Stent thrombosis numerically higher with no aspirin (NS)
- Meta-analyses show ~30% increase in ST without aspirin

### How Long to Continue Dual Therapy?
- AUGUSTUS: 6 months
- Real world: variable (1-12 months)
- Balance: bleeding risk vs ischemic risk

### Which P2Y12 Inhibitor?
- AUGUSTUS: 92% clopidogrel
- Ticagrelor/prasugrel with DOAC = high bleeding risk
- Most experts favor clopidogrel in this population

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Drop the aspirin** — biggest bleeding reduction (NNT = 14)
2. **Apixaban over warfarin** — 31% less bleeding, fewer hospitalizations
3. **Clopidogrel preferred** — ticagrelor/prasugrel + DOAC = bleeding nightmare
4. **Triple therapy: brief only** — 1 week max in most patients
5. **7.3% bleeding** with optimal regimen — still not trivial

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> AUGUSTUS answered the triple therapy question definitively: **Apixaban + P2Y12 inhibitor WITHOUT aspirin** is the safest regimen for AF patients after ACS/PCI. Aspirin adds bleeding (NNH = 14) without clear ischemic benefit. Warfarin is inferior to apixaban. Keep triple therapy to **days, not weeks**, then drop aspirin. Most patients should be on **DOAC + clopidogrel** for 1-12 months, then DOAC alone.

> **The conversation:** "You have two problems — atrial fibrillation and heart artery disease. We used to put everyone on three blood thinners, but that caused too much bleeding. Now we know that two is usually enough — a DOAC like apixaban plus a pill like clopidogrel. We'll drop the aspirin early to reduce your bleeding risk without increasing heart attack risk."
