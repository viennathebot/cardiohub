# DAPA-HF

**Dapagliflozin and Prevention of Adverse Outcomes in Heart Failure**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2019 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1911303 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT03036124 |
| **Bottom Line** | Dapagliflozin reduced death and HF hospitalization in HFrEF — with or without diabetes |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled |
| **N** | 4,744 |
| **Centers** | 410 sites, 20 countries |
| **Population** | HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%), NYHA II-IV, elevated NT-proBNP |
| **Mean Age** | 66 years |
| **Mean LVEF** | 31% |
| **Diabetes** | 42% had T2DM |
| **Median Follow-up** | 18.2 months |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Feature
- **Diabetes NOT required** — tested SGLT2i as HF drug, not diabetes drug
- First SGLT2i trial designed primarily for HF

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Dapagliflozin | 2,373 | 10 mg daily |
| Placebo | 2,371 | Matching placebo |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of worsening HF (hospitalization or urgent IV diuretic visit) or CV death**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- HF hospitalization or CV death
- All-cause mortality
- KCCQ symptom score
- Worsening renal function

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint

| Outcome | Dapagliflozin | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|---------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Worsening HF or CV death** | **16.3%** | **21.2%** | **0.74 (0.65-0.85)** | **<0.001** |

**26% relative risk reduction**

### Components

| Outcome | Dapagliflozin | Placebo | HR |
|---------|---------------|---------|-----|
| Worsening HF | 10.0% | 13.7% | 0.70 |
| CV death | 9.6% | 11.5% | 0.82 |
| **All-cause death** | **11.6%** | **13.9%** | **0.83 (p=0.022)** |

### NNT Calculations

| Outcome | ARR | NNT |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Primary endpoint | 4.9% | 20 |
| All-cause death | 2.3% | 43 |

### By Diabetes Status

| Subgroup | With Diabetes | Without Diabetes | Interaction |
|----------|---------------|------------------|-------------|
| Primary endpoint HR | 0.75 | 0.73 | **No interaction (p=0.80)** |

**Benefit independent of diabetes — SGLT2i is a HF drug, not just a diabetes drug**

### Symptom Improvement (KCCQ)

| Metric | Dapagliflozin | Placebo | P-value |
|--------|---------------|---------|---------|
| KCCQ improvement ≥5 points | 58% | 51% | <0.001 |
| KCCQ worsening ≥5 points | 25% | 33% | <0.001 |

## Safety

| Adverse Event | Dapagliflozin | Placebo |
|---------------|---------------|---------|
| Volume depletion | 7.5% | 6.8% |
| Renal adverse events | 6.5% | 7.2% |
| Diabetic ketoacidosis | 0.1% | 0 |
| Amputation | 0.5% | 0.5% |
| Fournier's gangrene | 0 | 0 |

**Remarkably safe — fewer adverse events than expected**

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **New HF drug class** — SGLT2i approved for HFrEF (not just diabetes)
2. **Diabetes not required** — expands eligible population
3. **Simple to use** — once daily, no titration
4. **Few AEs** — well-tolerated
5. **Four pillars complete** — ARNI + BB + MRA + SGLT2i

### FDA Approval
- Dapagliflozin approved for HFrEF (May 2020)
- Empagliflozin followed (EMPEROR-Reduced)
- First SGLT2i approved independent of diabetes

### Current Guidelines (2022)

| Recommendation | Class |
|----------------|-------|
| SGLT2i in HFrEF | **Class I** |
| SGLT2i in HFpEF | **Class IIa** |
| Regardless of diabetes status | Yes |

## The Four Pillars of HFrEF

| Drug Class | Example | Trial | Mortality Benefit |
|------------|---------|-------|-------------------|
| ARNI | Sacubitril/valsartan | PARADIGM-HF | Yes |
| Beta-blocker | Carvedilol | COPERNICUS | Yes |
| MRA | Spironolactone | RALES | Yes |
| **SGLT2i** | **Dapagliflozin** | **DAPA-HF** | **Yes** |

**All four reduce mortality — all should be initiated**

## Mechanism (Why SGLT2i Works in HF)

Not fully understood, but hypotheses include:
1. **Natriuresis/diuresis** — volume reduction
2. **Cardiac efficiency** — shifts fuel from fatty acids to ketones
3. **Anti-inflammatory** — reduced cardiac stress
4. **Nephroprotection** — slows CKD progression
5. **Autonomic modulation** — sympathetic inhibition

---

## Practical Considerations

### Starting SGLT2i in HFrEF
- No titration needed — 10 mg daily
- Check renal function (avoid if eGFR <20-25)
- Warn about genital infections (especially women)
- Hold if NPO or sick day (DKA risk in diabetics)
- Can cause initial eGFR dip (not harmful)

### When to Add
- Guidelines say add early — one of four pillars
- Can initiate in hospital (EMPULSE, SOLOIST-WHF)
- Benefits seen within days

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Diabetes not required** — SGLT2i works in all HFrEF
2. **NNT = 20** — very effective for HF endpoint
3. **All-cause mortality benefit** — 17% reduction
4. **Simple dosing** — 10 mg once daily, no titration
5. **Start early** — can initiate in hospital

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> DAPA-HF proved that **SGLT2i is a heart failure drug**, not just a diabetes drug. The benefit is consistent regardless of diabetes status. With a **NNT of 20** and proven mortality benefit, dapagliflozin (and empagliflozin) are now foundational therapy for HFrEF alongside ARNI, beta-blockers, and MRAs. Every HFrEF patient should be on an SGLT2i unless contraindicated.
