# ISCHEMIA

**International Study of Comparative Health Effectiveness with Medical and Invasive Approaches**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2020 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1915922 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01471522 |
| **Bottom Line** | Routine invasive strategy did NOT reduce death or MI vs conservative strategy in stable CAD with moderate-severe ischemia |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 5,179 |
| **Centers** | 320 sites, 37 countries |
| **Population** | Stable CAD with moderate-severe ischemia on stress testing |
| **Mean Age** | 64 years |
| **Median Follow-up** | 3.2 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Inclusion
- Moderate-severe ischemia on stress imaging (≥10% myocardium) or stress ECG
- Stable symptoms (no ACS in past 2 months)
- Able to undergo revascularization

### Key Exclusion
- **Left main ≥50%** (excluded by CCTA — critical design feature)
- LVEF <35%
- NYHA III-IV heart failure
- Unacceptable angina despite maximal medical therapy

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Invasive | 2,588 | Angiography + revascularization (PCI or CABG) + OMT |
| Conservative | 2,591 | OMT alone (cath only if medical therapy fails) |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of CV death, MI, hospitalization for unstable angina, heart failure, or resuscitated cardiac arrest**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- CV death or MI
- All-cause death
- Angina-related quality of life

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (3.2 Years)

| Outcome | Invasive | Conservative | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|----------|--------------|-------------|---------|
| **Primary composite** | **13.3%** | **15.5%** | **0.93 (0.80-1.08)** | **NS** |

**No significant difference**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | Invasive | Conservative | HR |
|---------|----------|--------------|-----|
| CV death | 2.9% | 3.0% | NS |
| MI | 8.1% | 9.4% | NS (periprocedural MIs in invasive) |
| Unstable angina hospitalization | 2.2% | 3.9% | 0.56 |
| Heart failure | 1.8% | 2.2% | NS |

### MI Breakdown (Critical Finding)

| MI Type | Invasive | Conservative |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Periprocedural (Type 4a/5) | 5.3% | 0.5% |
| Spontaneous (Type 1) | 3.4% | 5.6% |

**Invasive strategy: more periprocedural MI, fewer spontaneous MI — wash**

### Angina and Quality of Life

| Outcome | Invasive | Conservative |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Angina-free at 1 year | 50% | 38% |
| Angina-free at 3 years | 55% | 49% |
| SAQ improvement (1 year) | +8.5 points | +5.3 points |

**Invasive strategy improved angina — consistent benefit**

---

## Crossover

| Direction | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Conservative → Invasive | 23% (over trial) |
| Invasive → Conservative | <1% |

**1 in 4 conservative patients eventually got cath** — usually for refractory symptoms

---

## Subgroup Analyses

### No Subgroup Showed Clear Benefit

| Subgroup | Invasive Better? |
|----------|------------------|
| More severe ischemia | No |
| Diabetes | No |
| Multivessel CAD | No |
| Proximal LAD | No |
| Higher angina burden | No (but more symptom relief) |

### Possible Signal
- Patients with **no angina at baseline** — trend toward harm with invasive
- Patients with **daily angina** — trend toward benefit (symptom relief)

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **Challenged "oculostenotic reflex"** — seeing a stenosis doesn't mean you must fix it
2. **OMT is powerful** — conservative strategy had excellent outcomes
3. **Shared decision-making** — angina relief vs procedural risk conversation
4. **Guidelines updated** — revascularization for symptoms, not to prevent events

### What Didn't Change
- ACS still requires invasive strategy
- Left main disease excluded (ISCHEMIA not applicable)
- Symptoms matter — angina relief is real benefit

---

## Reconciling ISCHEMIA with FAME 2

| Factor | ISCHEMIA | FAME 2 |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Population | Moderate-severe ischemia (imaging) | FFR ≤0.80 (invasive) |
| Left main | Excluded by CCTA | Excluded |
| Ischemia proof | Stress test | FFR |
| Control arm | OMT (well-optimized) | OMT |
| Result | Neutral | Positive for PCI |
| Interpretation | Imaging ischemia ≠ FFR ischemia | FFR-confirmed lesions benefit |

**The synthesis:** Anatomic/imaging ischemia doesn't guarantee PCI benefit; FFR-proven ischemia does.

---

## Limitations

1. **Left main excluded** — can't apply to left main disease
2. **Moderate ischemia threshold** — what about mild ischemia?
3. **CCTA screening** — excluded 15% for left main pre-randomization
4. **High crossover** — 23% conservative patients got cath
5. **Underpowered for mortality** — would need 20,000+ patients

---

## Controversies

### "The Trial Was Negative"
- Critics: Practice changed for nothing
- Counter: Practice SHOULD change — stop reflexive revascularization

### "But My Patient Has a 90% LAD Lesion"
- ISCHEMIA included severe stenoses
- Proximity and severity didn't predict benefit
- Counter: FFR might identify the subset that benefits (FAME 2)

### Periprocedural MI Trade-off
- Invasive arm had MORE total MIs (periprocedural)
- Fewer spontaneous MIs — so roughly balanced
- But periprocedural MIs may have different prognostic weight

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **OMT first** — stable CAD with ischemia should start with medical therapy
2. **Symptoms drive revascularization** — not ischemia extent
3. **Left main excluded** — ISCHEMIA doesn't apply to significant left main
4. **FFR different from imaging** — FAME 2 still valid for FFR-positive lesions
5. **Shared decision-making** — "revascularization helps angina, doesn't prevent MI"

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> ISCHEMIA proved that **moderate-severe ischemia on stress testing is not an automatic revascularization trigger**. In stable CAD (excluding left main), OMT is non-inferior to routine invasive strategy for death and MI — but invasive strategy relieves angina better. Revascularize for symptoms, not stress test results.

> **The conversation:** "Your stress test shows ischemia, but studies show that fixing the blockages doesn't prevent heart attacks. It can help chest pain. Medical therapy alone is also safe. What matters more to you?"
