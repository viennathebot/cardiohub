# PLATO

**Platelet Inhibition and Patient Outcomes**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2009 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa0904327 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT00391872 |
| **Bottom Line** | Ticagrelor superior to clopidogrel in ACS — reduces death and MI without major bleeding increase |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, double-dummy |
| **N** | 18,624 |
| **Centers** | 862 sites, 43 countries |
| **Population** | ACS (STEMI or NSTEMI/UA) within 24 hours of symptom onset |
| **Mean Age** | 62 years |
| **Median Follow-up** | 9.1 months |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Ticagrelor | 9,333 | 180mg load, then 90mg BID |
| Clopidogrel | 9,291 | 300-600mg load, then 75mg daily |

### Double-Dummy Design
- Both groups got matching placebo
- True blinding despite different dosing schedules

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of CV death, MI, or stroke at 12 months**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause death
- CV death
- MI
- Stent thrombosis
- Definite stent thrombosis (ARC)

### Safety Endpoint
- PLATO major bleeding

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (12 Months)

| Outcome | Ticagrelor | Clopidogrel | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|------------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **CV death/MI/stroke** | **9.8%** | **11.7%** | **0.84 (0.77-0.92)** | **<0.001** |

**ARR = 1.9%** | **NNT = 53**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | Ticagrelor | Clopidogrel | HR | P-value |
|---------|------------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **CV death** | **4.0%** | **5.1%** | **0.79** | **<0.001** |
| MI | 5.8% | 6.9% | 0.84 | 0.005 |
| Stroke | 1.5% | 1.3% | NS | NS |
| **All-cause death** | **4.5%** | **5.9%** | **0.78** | **<0.001** |

### Stent Thrombosis

| Outcome | Ticagrelor | Clopidogrel | HR | P-value |
|---------|------------|-------------|-----|---------|
| Definite ST | 1.3% | 1.9% | 0.67 | <0.001 |
| Definite/probable ST | 2.2% | 2.9% | 0.75 | 0.01 |

**32% reduction in stent thrombosis**

## Safety

### Bleeding

| Outcome | Ticagrelor | Clopidogrel | HR | P-value |
|---------|------------|-------------|-----|---------|
| PLATO major bleeding | 11.6% | 11.2% | NS | NS |
| TIMI major bleeding | 7.9% | 7.7% | NS | NS |
| Life-threatening | 5.8% | 5.8% | NS | NS |
| **Non-CABG major** | **4.5%** | **3.8%** | **1.19** | **0.03** |

**More non-CABG bleeding with ticagrelor**

### Other Adverse Events

| Event | Ticagrelor | Clopidogrel |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| **Dyspnea** | **13.8%** | **7.8%** |
| Ventricular pauses >3s (Holter) | 5.8% | 3.6% |
| Uric acid elevation | Higher | — |

---

## The Dyspnea Story

### Characteristics

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Incidence | 13.8% (vs 7.8% clopidogrel) |
| Onset | Usually within first week |
| Severity | Usually mild-moderate |
| Discontinuation rate | ~1% |
| Mechanism | Adenosine-mediated |

### Counseling Points
- "You may feel short of breath — this is a known side effect"
- "It's not dangerous and usually improves"
- "Most patients tolerate it and don't need to stop"

## Subgroup Analyses

### The North American Paradox

| Region | Ticagrelor Effect |
|--------|-------------------|
| Rest of world | Clear benefit |
| **North America** | **No benefit (possible harm)** |

### Post-hoc Analysis: Aspirin Dose

| ASA Dose | Ticagrelor Effect |
|----------|-------------------|
| <100mg | Benefit |
| ≥100mg | Attenuated/absent |
| **North America pattern** | Higher ASA doses |

**FDA Label:** Avoid aspirin >100mg/day with ticagrelor

---

## Ticagrelor Pharmacology

### Why Ticagrelor May Be Better

| Property | Ticagrelor | Clopidogrel |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Prodrug | No | Yes (requires CYP2C19) |
| Reversible | Yes | No |
| Onset | 30 min | 2-4 hours |
| Offset | ~12 hours | 5-7 days |
| Genetic variability | Minimal | CYP2C19 dependent |

### CYP2C19 Loss-of-Function
- ~30% of population
- Clopidogrel non-response
- Ticagrelor not affected
- May explain ticagrelor superiority

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **Ticagrelor became first-line** — preferred over clopidogrel in ACS
2. **Low-dose aspirin mandate** — ≤100mg with ticagrelor
3. **Mortality benefit** — rare in antiplatelet trials
4. **BID dosing accepted** — despite inconvenience

### Guideline Recommendations (ACC/AHA 2023)

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| ACS (PCI or medical) | Ticagrelor preferred (Class I) |
| STEMI | Ticagrelor or prasugrel (Class I) |
| Aspirin dose | ≤100mg with ticagrelor (Class III for higher) |

---

## Limitations

1. **Double-dummy complexity** — elaborate blinding
2. **North American results** — unexplained discrepancy
3. **Dyspnea common** — impacts tolerability
4. **BID dosing** — compliance concerns
5. **Cost** — more expensive than clopidogrel

---

## Controversies

### North American Paradox
- Never fully explained
- Aspirin dose hypothesis post-hoc
- FDA required low-dose aspirin label
- Some skepticism about trial integrity

### Real-World Adherence
- BID dosing may reduce compliance
- Dyspnea causes discontinuation
- Clopidogrel still widely used

### Who Gets Clopidogrel Instead?
- Need anticoagulation (triple therapy)
- Oral surgery planned
- Prior ICH
- Strong aspirin dose preference
- Cost/access issues

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Mortality benefit** — ticagrelor reduces all-cause death (NNT ~77)
2. **Low-dose aspirin MANDATORY** — ≤100mg daily
3. **Dyspnea is common** — warn patients, usually tolerable
4. **BID dosing** — 90mg twice daily
5. **No genetic variability** — works regardless of CYP2C19 status

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> PLATO established ticagrelor as the preferred P2Y12 inhibitor in ACS by demonstrating **reduced CV death, MI, and all-cause mortality** compared to clopidogrel — without increasing major bleeding. The NNT of 53 for the primary endpoint and ~77 for death are clinically meaningful. **Counsel patients about dyspnea** and ensure **aspirin is ≤100mg daily**. Ticagrelor's reversibility and lack of CYP2C19 dependence make it pharmacologically attractive.

> **The conversation:** "This blood thinner works faster and more reliably than clopidogrel. It reduces death and heart attacks. You'll take it twice a day. Some people feel short of breath — this is a known side effect, usually mild, and not dangerous. It's important to take low-dose aspirin with it — high-dose aspirin reduces the benefit."
