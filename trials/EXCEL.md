# EXCEL

**Evaluation of XIENCE versus Coronary Artery Bypass Surgery for Effectiveness of Left Main Revascularization**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2016 (3-year), 2019 (5-year) |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1610227 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01205776 |
| **Bottom Line** | PCI non-inferior to CABG for left main at 3 years; 5-year data sparked controversy |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 1,905 |
| **Centers** | 126 sites worldwide |
| **Population** | Left main CAD with low-intermediate SYNTAX score (≤32) |
| **Mean Age** | 66 years |
| **Mean SYNTAX Score** | 20.6 |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Inclusion
- Significant left main stenosis (≥70% visual or ≥50% with ischemia/FFR)
- SYNTAX score ≤32 (low-intermediate complexity)
- Heart Team consensus for equipoise

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| PCI | 948 | XIENCE everolimus-eluting stent |
| CABG | 957 | Coronary artery bypass grafting |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of death, stroke, or MI at 3 years**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- Individual components
- Revascularization (ischemia-driven)
- MACCE (death, stroke, MI, or ischemia-driven revasc)

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (3 Years) — Initial Publication

| Outcome | PCI | CABG | Difference | P (non-inferiority) |
|---------|-----|------|------------|---------------------|
| **Death, stroke, or MI** | **15.4%** | **14.7%** | **+0.7%** | **0.02 (met)** |

**PCI non-inferior to CABG**

### Component Analysis (3 Years)

| Outcome | PCI | CABG | HR (95% CI) |
|---------|-----|------|-------------|
| Death | 8.2% | 5.9% | 1.39 (0.98-1.96) |
| Stroke | 2.3% | 2.9% | 0.78 (0.46-1.31) |
| MI | 8.1% | 8.0% | 1.01 (0.71-1.44) |

### Revascularization (3 Years)

| Outcome | PCI | CABG |
|---------|-----|------|
| Any revascularization | 12.6% | 7.5% |
| Ischemia-driven revasc | 9.0% | 4.8% |

---

## The 5-Year Controversy

### 5-Year Primary Endpoint

| Outcome | PCI | CABG | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|-------------|---------|
| **Death, stroke, or MI** | **22.0%** | **19.2%** | **1.19 (0.95-1.50)** | **0.13** |

**Non-inferiority no longer met at 5 years**

### 5-Year Components

| Outcome | PCI | CABG | HR | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|-----|---------|
| **Death** | **13.0%** | **9.9%** | **1.38** | **0.046** |
| Stroke | 2.9% | 3.7% | NS | — |
| MI | 10.6% | 9.1% | NS | — |
| Ischemia-driven revasc | 16.9% | 10.0% | <0.001 | — |

---

## The MI Definition War

### Why This Trial Exploded

**The crux:** Definition of periprocedural MI

| Definition | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| **Trial protocol MI** | Universal Definition, CABG-biased (higher troponin threshold for CABG) |
| **SCAI Definition** | More CABG MIs captured |
| **SYNTAX MI Definition** | Different again |

### The Analysis That Changed Everything

When MIs were adjudicated using **SCAI criteria** (proposed as fairer):
- CABG arm had MORE periprocedural MIs
- PCI looked better at 5 years

### What the Critics Said
1. Abbott (sponsor) accused of selective endpoint definitions
2. Some CABG surgeons demanded retraction
3. NEJM published critical letters and rebuttals
4. Led to European guideline downgrade of PCI for left main

---

## The NOBLE Trial (Parallel)

Published same time, different result:

| Trial | N | 5-Year MACCE | Result |
|-------|---|--------------|--------|
| EXCEL | 1,905 | PCI 22% vs CABG 19% | Non-inferior at 3Y, not 5Y |
| NOBLE | 1,201 | PCI 29% vs CABG 19% | CABG superior |

**NOBLE used different stent (Biolimus), different MI definition, included all SYNTAX scores**

---

## Clinical Impact

### Guideline Evolution

| Year | ACC/AHA | ESC |
|------|---------|-----|
| 2018 | PCI Class IIa for SYNTAX ≤22 | PCI Class I for SYNTAX ≤22 |
| 2021 | PCI Class IIb for most left main | PCI downgraded, CABG preferred |

### Current Practice
- **SYNTAX 0-22:** PCI reasonable (Class IIa)
- **SYNTAX 23-32:** CABG preferred, PCI selectively
- **SYNTAX >32:** CABG strongly preferred
- **Heart Team** discussion mandatory

---

## Limitations

1. **Industry-sponsored** — Abbott funded; allegations of endpoint manipulation
2. **MI definition changes** — protocol amendments raised red flags
3. **SYNTAX ≤32 only** — doesn't apply to complex left main
4. **Lost to follow-up** — 10%+ at 5 years
5. **Composite endpoint** — masks component differences

---

## Controversies

### The Retraction Demand
- Letter signed by 27 surgeons calling for retraction
- Allegations of data manipulation
- NEJM conducted investigation, did not retract
- Remains highly contentious

### Mortality Signal
- 13.0% vs 9.9% death at 5 years (p=0.046)
- Critics: "PCI kills patients"
- Supporters: "Within non-inferiority margin for composite"
- Reality: Unclear if finding is real or statistical noise

### Why It Matters
- Left main = highest stakes decision in PCI
- Wrong choice = potential death
- Both camps (surgery vs PCI) heavily invested

---

## What We Actually Learned

1. **PCI is a viable option** for low-complexity left main (SYNTAX ≤22)
2. **CABG remains preferred** for complex left main
3. **Heart Team is essential** — not a one-person decision
4. **Long-term follow-up matters** — 3-year data insufficient
5. **MI definitions matter** — affects trial interpretation

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **SYNTAX score drives decision** — ≤22 favors PCI, >32 favors CABG
2. **5-year data changed guidelines** — can't ignore late divergence
3. **Revasc rates higher with PCI** — 16.9% vs 10.0% at 5 years
4. **Stroke slightly lower with PCI** — 2.9% vs 3.7% (NS but consistent)
5. **Don't cite EXCEL without caveat** — it's a lightning rod

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> EXCEL showed that **PCI can work for left main** — but with caveats. Low SYNTAX scores (≤22), good anatomy, patient preference, and surgical risk all factor in. The 5-year divergence and controversy mean **CABG remains default for most left main disease**, with PCI as a reasonable alternative in selected patients after Heart Team discussion.

> **The meta-lesson:** Be skeptical of industry-sponsored trials with changing endpoints. Let the dust settle before changing practice.
