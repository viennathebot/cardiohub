# CASTLE-AF

**Catheter Ablation versus Standard Conventional Therapy in Patients with Left Ventricular Dysfunction and Atrial Fibrillation**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2018 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1707855 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT00643188 |
| **Bottom Line** | AF ablation reduces mortality and HF hospitalization in HFrEF patients with AF |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 363 |
| **Centers** | 33 sites, Europe and USA |
| **Population** | Symptomatic paroxysmal/persistent AF with HFrEF (LVEF ≤35%) and ICD/CRT-D |
| **Mean Age** | 64 years |
| **Mean LVEF** | 25% |
| **Median Follow-up** | 37.8 months |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Inclusion
- Paroxysmal or persistent AF
- LVEF ≤35%
- NYHA II-IV heart failure
- ICD or CRT-D implanted
- Failed or intolerant to ≥1 antiarrhythmic drug

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Ablation | 179 | Pulmonary vein isolation + medical therapy |
| Medical | 184 | Rate/rhythm control (drugs only) |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of all-cause death or HF hospitalization**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause mortality
- HF hospitalization
- CV death
- AF burden (ICD-detected)
- LVEF change
- 6-minute walk distance

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint

| Outcome | Ablation | Medical | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|----------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Death or HF hospitalization** | **28.5%** | **44.6%** | **0.62 (0.43-0.87)** | **0.007** |

**ARR = 16.1%** | **NNT = 6**

### Secondary Endpoints

| Outcome | Ablation | Medical | HR | P-value |
|---------|----------|---------|-----|---------|
| **All-cause death** | **13.4%** | **25.0%** | **0.53** | **0.01** |
| **CV death** | **11.2%** | **22.3%** | **0.49** | **0.009** |
| HF hospitalization | 20.7% | 35.9% | 0.56 | 0.004 |

### NNT Analysis

| Endpoint | NNT |
|----------|-----|
| Death or HF hospitalization | **6** |
| All-cause death | **8** |
| HF hospitalization | **7** |

## Why CASTLE-AF Is Landmark

### First Mortality Benefit for AF Ablation

| Previous Trial | Population | Result |
|----------------|------------|--------|
| AFFIRM | General AF | Rate = rhythm |
| CABANA | All-comers AF | ITT neutral |
| AATAC | HFrEF + AF | Positive (secondary) |
| **CASTLE-AF** | HFrEF + AF | **Mortality benefit** |

### Why HFrEF Patients Benefit More
- AF hemodynamic impact worse in failing hearts
- Loss of atrial kick critical when EF low
- Tachycardia-mediated cardiomyopathy common
- Rate control alone inadequate

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **AF ablation guidelines upgraded** — Class IIa for HFrEF + AF
2. **Ablation as HF therapy** — not just arrhythmia therapy
3. **Device monitoring valuable** — ICD tracking AF burden
4. **Rhythm control strategy** — favored in HFrEF

### Guideline Recommendations

| Society | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| ACC/AHA 2023 | AF ablation Class IIa for HFrEF |
| ESC 2020 HF | AF ablation Class IIa for tachycardia-induced CMP |
| HRS 2024 | AF ablation to improve outcomes in HFrEF |

## CASTLE-AF vs CABANA

### Why Different Results?

| Parameter | CASTLE-AF | CABANA |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Population | HFrEF (LVEF ≤35%) | All-comers AF |
| N | 363 | 2,204 |
| Mean LVEF | 25% | 55% |
| Primary result | **Positive** | ITT neutral |
| Mortality | **Reduced** | NS |
| Interpretation | HFrEF benefits | General AF neutral |

**CASTLE-AF population had more to gain from sinus rhythm**

---

## Patient Selection for AF Ablation in HFrEF

### Ideal Candidates
- [ ] HFrEF with LVEF ≤35%
- [ ] Symptomatic paroxysmal/persistent AF
- [ ] Suitable anatomy for ablation
- [ ] Failed/intolerant of medical therapy
- [ ] ICD/CRT-D in place
- [ ] Expected to tolerate procedure

### When to Consider Alternatives
- Long-standing persistent AF (>3 years)
- Large left atrium (>55mm)
- End-stage HF (LVAD candidate)
- Multiple prior failed ablations
- High procedural risk

## The Tachycardia-Mediated Cardiomyopathy Angle

### LVEF Recovery Potential

| Scenario | LVEF Recovery Expected |
|----------|----------------------|
| AF + low LVEF, unclear etiology | High |
| Known ischemic CMP + AF | Moderate |
| Long-standing DCM + AF | Variable |
| Preserved LV + AF | Less relevant |

**CASTLE-AF demonstrated 8% absolute LVEF improvement**

---

## Limitations

1. **Small sample size** — 363 patients
2. **Selected population** — all had ICD/CRT-D
3. **Open-label** — no sham control
4. **Long enrollment** — 10 years to enroll
5. **Single ablation strategy** — PVI only

---

## Controversies

### Can Results Be Generalized?
- ICD/CRT-D population specific
- Very low LVEF (mean 25%)
- Would benefit extend to LVEF 35-50%?

### Why Was Enrollment So Slow?
- 10 years for 363 patients
- Operator preference, selection bias
- May limit generalizability

### Is It the Ablation or the Rhythm?
- AFFIRM: Rate ≈ rhythm
- CASTLE-AF: Ablation > medical rhythm control
- Ablation maintains sinus better than drugs

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **NNT = 8 for death** — remarkably low
2. **LVEF improves by 8%** — ablation is HF therapy
3. **ICD-detected AF burden** — good monitoring metric
4. **HFrEF is the sweet spot** — low EF + AF = ablation benefit
5. **Don't delay** — earlier ablation likely better

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> CASTLE-AF proved that in **HFrEF patients with AF**, catheter ablation **reduces mortality** (NNT = 8) and HF hospitalizations. This was the first trial to demonstrate a mortality benefit for AF ablation, validating the hypothesis that **rhythm control matters most in failing hearts**. The **8% LVEF improvement** suggests AF ablation should be viewed as HF therapy, not just arrhythmia therapy. For eligible HFrEF patients with symptomatic AF, ablation should be actively considered.

> **The conversation:** "Your heart failure and atrial fibrillation are making each other worse. Studies show that treating the irregular rhythm with a procedure called ablation can improve your heart function and reduce your risk of hospitalization and death. About 1 in 8 patients like you will benefit significantly."
