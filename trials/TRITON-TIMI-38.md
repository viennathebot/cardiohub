# TRITON-TIMI 38

**Trial to Assess Improvement in Therapeutic Outcomes by Optimizing Platelet Inhibition with Prasugrel**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2007 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa0706482 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT00097591 |
| **Bottom Line** | Prasugrel superior to clopidogrel in ACS with PCI — but higher bleeding, especially in certain subgroups |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind |
| **N** | 13,608 |
| **Centers** | 707 sites, 30 countries |
| **Population** | Moderate-to-high-risk ACS with planned PCI |
| **Mean Age** | 61 years |
| **Median Follow-up** | 14.5 months |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Design Features
- ACS patients with KNOWN coronary anatomy (post-angiography)
- All planned for PCI
- Not applicable to medically managed ACS

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Prasugrel | 6,813 | 60mg load, then 10mg daily |
| Clopidogrel | 6,795 | 300mg load, then 75mg daily |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of CV death, nonfatal MI, or nonfatal stroke**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- CV death, MI, stroke, or urgent TVR
- Stent thrombosis (definite or probable)
- Individual components

### Safety Endpoint
- TIMI major bleeding (non-CABG related)

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint

| Outcome | Prasugrel | Clopidogrel | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **CV death/MI/stroke** | **9.9%** | **12.1%** | **0.81 (0.73-0.90)** | **<0.001** |

**ARR = 2.2%** | **NNT = 46**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | Prasugrel | Clopidogrel | HR | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----|---------|
| CV death | 2.1% | 2.4% | NS | NS |
| **MI** | **7.3%** | **9.5%** | **0.76** | **<0.001** |
| Stroke | 1.0% | 1.0% | NS | NS |

### Stent Thrombosis — The Headline

| Outcome | Prasugrel | Clopidogrel | HR | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **Definite/probable ST** | **1.1%** | **2.4%** | **0.48** | **<0.001** |

**52% reduction in stent thrombosis**

---

## Safety

### Bleeding

| Outcome | Prasugrel | Clopidogrel | HR | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **TIMI major** | **2.4%** | **1.8%** | **1.32** | **0.03** |
| **Life-threatening** | **1.4%** | **0.9%** | **1.52** | **0.01** |
| **Fatal bleeding** | **0.4%** | **0.1%** | **4.19** | **0.002** |

**NNH for major bleeding = 167**

### Net Clinical Benefit

| Population | Net Benefit (Ischemia - Bleeding) |
|------------|-----------------------------------|
| Overall | Favors prasugrel |
| Prior TIA/stroke | **Harm** |
| Age ≥75 | Neutral |
| Weight <60kg | Neutral |

---

## The Subgroups That Changed Practice

### Prior TIA/Stroke — CONTRAINDICATED

| Outcome | Prasugrel | Clopidogrel | HR |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----|
| CV events | No benefit | — | NS |
| Stroke/TIA | 6.5% | 1.2% | **Harm** |
| Net clinical | **Harm** | — | — |

**FDA Black Box Warning:** Do not use in patients with prior stroke/TIA

### Elderly (≥75 Years)

| Outcome | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| Efficacy | Preserved |
| Bleeding | Increased |
| Net benefit | Neutral/marginal |

**Consider 5mg dose in elderly** (DAPT study supported this)

### Low Body Weight (<60 kg)

| Outcome | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| Efficacy | Preserved |
| Bleeding | Increased |
| Net benefit | Attenuated |

**Consider 5mg dose**

## TRITON vs PLATO

### Head-to-Head Comparison

| Parameter | TRITON (Prasugrel) | PLATO (Ticagrelor) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Population | ACS with PCI | All ACS |
| CV death | No reduction | **Reduced** |
| MI | Reduced | Reduced |
| Stent thrombosis | **-52%** | -32% |
| Major bleeding | Increased | Non-CABG only |
| Mortality | NS | **Reduced** |
| Contraindications | Prior TIA/stroke | Aspirin >100mg |
| Dosing | Once daily | Twice daily |

### Practical Distinctions
- **Prasugrel:** Strongest antiplatelet effect, best for high-risk anatomy (left main, bifurcation, long stent), avoid if prior stroke
- **Ticagrelor:** Mortality benefit, works in medical management, reversible, dyspnea

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **Prasugrel added to armamentarium** — option for high-risk PCI
2. **Contraindicated in prior stroke/TIA** — black box warning
3. **Subgroup-dependent prescribing** — weight, age considerations
4. **Superior stent thrombosis reduction** — attractive for complex PCI

### When to Choose Prasugrel Over Ticagrelor

| Favor Prasugrel | Favor Ticagrelor |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Complex PCI (high ST risk) | Prior TIA/stroke |
| No prior stroke/TIA | Medically managed ACS |
| Age <75, weight >60kg | Mortality priority |
| Once-daily preference | Unknown anatomy (pre-cath) |
| Dyspnea intolerance | Reversibility desired |

## Guideline Recommendations

### ACC/AHA 2023

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| STEMI with PCI | Prasugrel or ticagrelor (Class I) |
| NSTEMI with PCI | Prasugrel or ticagrelor (Class I) |
| Prior TIA/stroke | **Prasugrel contraindicated** |
| Medically managed | Ticagrelor preferred (no TRITON data) |

### ESC 2023

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| ACS with PCI | Prasugrel generally preferred over ticagrelor |
| STEMI | Prasugrel (Class I) |
| Contraindications | Prior stroke, high bleeding risk |

## Limitations

1. **PCI patients only** — no data for medical management
2. **Known anatomy required** — loaded after angiography
3. **Bleeding trade-off** — NNH 167 for major bleeding
4. **Cannot use in prior stroke** — significant exclusion
5. **Single-dose regimen** — no dose reduction arm in main trial

---

## Controversies

### Prasugrel vs Ticagrelor — Which Is Better?
- No head-to-head RCT
- ISAR-REACT 5: Prasugrel lower events in ACS with PCI
- But: ticagrelor has mortality benefit, reversibility, broader population
- ESC shifted toward prasugrel; ACC/AHA neutral

### The 5mg Dose Question
- TRITON showed harm in elderly/low weight
- 5mg maintenance studied in DAPT trial
- Reasonable option but not prospectively validated

### Pre-Treatment
- TRITON loaded AFTER angiography (known anatomy)
- Pre-treatment with prasugrel not supported
- ACCOAST: Pre-treatment prasugrel harmful

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **52% stent thrombosis reduction** — strongest antiplatelet effect
2. **Prior stroke/TIA = contraindicated** — BLACK BOX WARNING
3. **Once daily dosing** — compliance advantage over ticagrelor
4. **PCI patients only** — no data for medical management
5. **Consider 5mg** in elderly (≥75) or low weight (<60kg)

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> TRITON proved prasugrel is more potent than clopidogrel, with **52% reduction in stent thrombosis** and **NNT of 46** for ischemic events. But the **bleeding cost is real**: increased major, life-threatening, and fatal bleeding. The drug is **contraindicated in prior stroke/TIA** (black box) and should be used cautiously in the **elderly and low-weight patients**. Prasugrel is the right choice for **high-risk PCI anatomy** when bleeding risk is acceptable — but choose ticagrelor for prior stroke, medical management, or when reversibility matters.

> **Prescribing decision tree:**
> 1. Prior TIA/stroke? → NO prasugrel
> 2. Medical management? → ticagrelor
> 3. High-risk PCI, young, good weight? → prasugrel
> 4. Elderly/low weight? → consider ticagrelor or prasugrel 5mg
