# COAPT

**Cardiovascular Outcomes Assessment of the MitraClip Percutaneous Therapy for Heart Failure Patients with Functional Mitral Regurgitation**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2018 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1806640 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01626079 |
| **Bottom Line** | MitraClip + GDMT dramatically reduces mortality and HF hospitalizations vs GDMT alone in secondary MR with HFrEF |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 614 |
| **Centers** | 78 sites (US and Canada) |
| **Population** | Symptomatic HFrEF with moderate-severe or severe secondary MR despite GDMT |
| **Mean Age** | 72 years |
| **Mean LVEF** | 31% |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Inclusion Criteria
- LVEF 20-50%
- LVEDVI ≤ 96 mL/m² (not too dilated)
- EROA ≥ 30 mm² or RVol ≥ 45 mL (significant MR)
- Symptomatic (NYHA II-IVa) despite maximal GDMT
- Heart Team deems surgery high-risk

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Device | 302 | MitraClip + continued GDMT |
| Control | 312 | GDMT alone |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoints
1. **Efficacy:** HF hospitalizations at 24 months (analyzed as recurrent events)
2. **Safety:** Freedom from device-related complications at 12 months

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause mortality at 24 months
- Quality of life (KCCQ)
- 6-minute walk distance
- NYHA class improvement

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint: HF Hospitalizations (24 Months)

| Outcome | MitraClip | Control | Rate Ratio | P-value |
|---------|-----------|---------|------------|---------|
| **HF hospitalizations per patient-year** | **35.8%** | **67.9%** | **0.53** | **<0.001** |

**Annualized rate:** 35.8 vs 67.9 per 100 patient-years

### All-Cause Mortality (24 Months)

| Outcome | MitraClip | Control | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Death from any cause** | **29.1%** | **46.1%** | **0.62 (0.46-0.82)** | **<0.001** |

**ARR = 17%** | **NNT = 6** (treat 6 patients to save 1 life at 2 years)

### Combined Endpoint

| Outcome | MitraClip | Control | HR | P-value |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----|---------|
| Death or HF hospitalization | 45.7% | 67.9% | 0.53 | <0.001 |

### Quality of Life (KCCQ at 12 Months)

| Metric | MitraClip | Control | Difference |
|--------|-----------|---------|------------|
| KCCQ overall summary | +12.5 points | -1.0 points | **+13.5 points** |

(>5 point change is clinically meaningful)

### Functional Status

| Metric | MitraClip | Control |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| NYHA I or II at 12 months | 72.2% | 49.6% |

## Why COAPT Worked (vs MITRA-FR)

### The Proportionality Hypothesis

| Parameter | COAPT | MITRA-FR |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| EROA (mean) | 41 mm² | 31 mm² |
| LVEDVI (mean) | 101 mL/m² | 135 mL/m² |
| MR severity | More severe | Less severe |
| LV dilation | Less dilated | More dilated |

**COAPT enrolled patients with "disproportionate" MR:**
- MR severity OUT OF PROPORTION to LV dilation
- These patients have MR driving HF (fixable target)

**MITRA-FR enrolled patients with "proportionate" MR:**
- MR proportional to LV dilation  
- These patients have HF driving MR (fixing MR doesn't help)

### The Grayken Formula

> **EROA / LVEDVI ratio**
> - COAPT: 41/101 = 0.41
> - MITRA-FR: 31/135 = 0.23
> 
> Higher ratio = disproportionate MR = MitraClip benefit

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **FDA expanded indication** (March 2019) — MitraClip approved for secondary MR in HFrEF
2. **Class IIa recommendation** in ACC/AHA guidelines for carefully selected patients
3. **Heart Failure teams** now routinely assess MR as treatment target
4. **Patient selection** became critical — not all secondary MR benefits

### The Selection Checklist (COAPT Criteria)
- [ ] LVEF 20-50%
- [ ] LVEDVI ≤ 96 mL/m² (not too dilated)
- [ ] Moderate-severe or severe MR (EROA ≥30, RVol ≥45)
- [ ] Symptomatic despite GDMT
- [ ] Anatomically suitable for MitraClip
- [ ] Not end-stage (cardiac index >2.0, PASP <70)

---

## Limitations

1. **Highly selected population** — only ~5% of screened patients enrolled
2. **Open-label design** — no sham procedure (placebo effect possible)
3. **US/Canada only** — may not generalize to all healthcare systems
4. **Industry-sponsored** — Abbott funded the trial
5. **GDMT optimization** — control arm may not have been on maximal therapy (some critics)

---

## Controversies

### COAPT vs MITRA-FR
- Why did COAPT show benefit but MITRA-FR (published same day) showed none?
- Proportionality hypothesis now widely accepted
- Debates continue about optimal selection criteria

### "Optimal GDMT" Definition
- Critics argue control patients weren't truly optimized
- Counter: Real-world HF patients often aren't on max doses
- Pragmatic trial reflects reality

### Cost and Access
- MitraClip procedure: ~$35,000-50,000
- Cost-effective given mortality benefit
- Access limited to high-volume structural heart centers

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> COAPT proved that **the right MR in the right patient** dramatically improves outcomes. The key is **proportionality** — look for significant MR (EROA ≥30 mm²) in a ventricle that's not too dilated (LVEDVI ≤96 mL/m²). These patients have MR driving their HF, not HF driving their MR. Fix the MR, fix the patient.

> **The NNT of 6 to save a life** is remarkable — comparable to the best mortality-reducing HF therapies.

| N | 614 | 304 |

| Mean LVEDVI | 101 mL/m² | 135 mL/m² |
| Primary endpoint | HF hospitalizations | Death or HF hospitalization |
| Mortality at 1 year | 19.1% vs 23.2% | 24.3% vs 22.4% |
| Mortality at 2 years | 29.1% vs 46.1% | — |
| Result | **Positive** | **Negative** |
| Interpretation | MR driving HF | HF driving MR |

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **NNT = 6** — one of the most impressive mortality benefits in cardiology
2. **Patient selection is everything** — COAPT criteria vs "any secondary MR"
3. **EROA/LVEDVI ratio >0.4** — rough rule for disproportionate MR
4. **Don't clip the dilated ventricle** — LVEDVI >96 mL/m² predicts futility
5. **KCCQ +13.5 points** — patients feel dramatically better
