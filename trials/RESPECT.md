# RESPECT

**Randomized Evaluation of Recurrent Stroke Comparing PFO Closure to Established Current Standard of Care Treatment**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2013 (primary), 2017 (extended follow-up) |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1301440 (primary), 10.1056/NEJMoa1610057 (extended) |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT00465270 |
| **Bottom Line** | PFO closure reduces recurrent stroke in cryptogenic stroke — confirmed on extended follow-up |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 980 |
| **Centers** | 69 US and Canadian sites |
| **Population** | Cryptogenic stroke age 18-60 with PFO |
| **Mean Age** | 46 years |
| **Median Follow-up** | 2.6 years (primary), 5.9 years (extended) |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Inclusion
- Cryptogenic ischemic stroke (no identified cause despite workup)
- PFO on TEE with right-to-left shunt
- Age 18-60 years
- No other cause of stroke identified

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| PFO Closure | 499 | Amplatzer PFO Occluder + medical therapy |
| Medical Therapy | 481 | Antiplatelet or anticoagulation (physician choice) |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of recurrent nonfatal ischemic stroke, fatal ischemic stroke, or early death**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- Recurrent stroke (any type)
- TIA
- New brain infarcts on imaging

---

## Results

### Initial Publication (2.6 Years Median Follow-up)

| Outcome | Closure | Medical | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|---------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Primary endpoint** | **1.8%** | **3.3%** | **0.49 (0.22-1.11)** | **0.08** |

**Trend favoring closure but NOT statistically significant**

### Extended Follow-up (5.9 Years Median)

| Outcome | Closure | Medical | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|---------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Recurrent stroke** | **3.6%** | **5.8%** | **0.55 (0.31-0.999)** | **0.046** |

**ARR = 2.2%** | **NNT = 45** (over 6 years)

### Per-Protocol Analysis (Extended)

| Outcome | Closure | Medical | HR | P-value |
|---------|---------|---------|-----|---------|
| Recurrent stroke | 2.0% | 6.1% | 0.27 | <0.001 |

**Even stronger effect in those who got assigned treatment**

## PFO Features That Predict Benefit

### High-Risk PFO Characteristics

| Feature | Associated with Benefit |
|---------|------------------------|
| Large shunt | Yes |
| Atrial septal aneurysm (ASA) | Yes |
| Hypermobile septum | Yes |
| RoPE score ≥7 | Yes |

### The RoPE Score

| Points | Factor |
|--------|--------|
| +1 | Age 18-29 |
| +1 each decade | Age younger |
| +1 | No hypertension |
| +1 | No diabetes |
| +1 | No prior stroke/TIA |
| +1 | Non-smoker |
| +1 | Cortical infarct |

**Higher RoPE score = more likely PFO is causal**

## Why Initial RESPECT Was "Negative"

### Lessons Learned

| Issue | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| Short follow-up | Stroke recurrence takes years |
| Low event rate | Hard to show difference |
| ITT dilution | 16% crossover to closure |
| Point estimate favored closure | HR 0.49, just not significant |

### What Extended Follow-up Showed
- Same patients, longer observation
- Events accumulated
- Statistical significance achieved
- Original hypothesis validated

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **FDA approval** — Amplatzer PFO Occluder approved (2016)
2. **Guidelines updated** — PFO closure now recommended
3. **Neurology/cardiology collaboration** — joint decision-making
4. **Patient selection refined** — RoPE score, PFO features

### Current Guidelines (AHA/ASA 2021)

| Recommendation | Class |
|----------------|-------|
| PFO closure in cryptogenic stroke age <60, high-risk PFO features | Class IIa |
| PFO closure considered vs medical therapy | Reasonable |
| Medical therapy if closure not possible | Class I |

---

## Patient Selection

### Ideal Closure Candidate
- [ ] Age <60 years
- [ ] Cryptogenic stroke (thorough workup negative)
- [ ] PFO with right-to-left shunt
- [ ] High-risk features (ASA, large shunt)
- [ ] RoPE score ≥7
- [ ] No other stroke etiology

### When to Avoid Closure
- Clearly cardioembolic (AF, LV thrombus)
- Small vessel disease
- Hypercoagulable state explaining stroke
- Age >60 (less benefit)
- Unable to tolerate antiplatelet therapy post-procedure

---

## Limitations

1. **Initial underpowering** — required extended follow-up
2. **Single device** — Amplatzer only
3. **Selected population** — young, cryptogenic only
4. **Crossover** — 16% medical arm got closure
5. **Open-label** — no blinding

---

## Controversies

### Age Cutoff
- Trials enrolled up to age 60
- Real-world: older patients request closure
- Less clear benefit >60 (competing stroke causes)

### Device Choice
- RESPECT: Amplatzer
- CLOSE: Multiple devices
- Clinical equivalence assumed but unproven

### Atrial Fibrillation Risk
- Device-related AF: 0.5-6.6%
- Most self-limited
- Long-term implications unclear

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Extended follow-up was key** — initial "negative" became positive
2. **NNT = 45 over 6 years** — modest but real benefit
3. **RoPE score ≥7** — strongest candidates
4. **Cryptogenic = thorough workup** — not "we didn't look hard"
5. **CLOSE was stronger** — if only one trial to cite, use CLOSE

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> RESPECT demonstrated that **PFO closure reduces recurrent stroke** in carefully selected patients with cryptogenic stroke. The initial "negative" result became positive with **extended follow-up**, teaching us that stroke prevention trials need time. Select patients using **RoPE score** and **PFO features**. PFO closure is now standard of care for young patients (<60) with cryptogenic stroke and high-risk PFO anatomy.

> **The conversation:** "Your stroke had no clear cause, and you have a hole between the upper chambers of your heart. Studies show that closing this hole reduces your risk of another stroke. The procedure has a small risk of complications, and there's a chance of temporary irregular heartbeat. But over 6 years, closing the hole prevents about 1 stroke for every 45 procedures."
