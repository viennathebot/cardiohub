# COMPASS

**Cardiovascular Outcomes for People Using Anticoagulation Strategies**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2017 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1709118 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01776424 |
| **Bottom Line** | Rivaroxaban 2.5 mg BID + aspirin superior to aspirin alone in stable atherosclerotic disease |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled |
| **N** | 27,395 |
| **Centers** | 602 sites, 33 countries |
| **Population** | Stable CAD or PAD |
| **Mean Age** | 68 years |
| **Median Follow-up** | 23 months |
| **Randomization** | 1:1:1 (three arms) |

### Key Population
- **Stable** atherosclerotic disease (no ACS in past month)
- CAD (prior MI, multivessel CAD, or prior CABG/PCI) OR
- PAD (symptomatic or prior intervention)

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| **Rivaroxaban + ASA** | **9,152** | **Rivaroxaban 2.5 mg BID + ASA 100 mg daily** |
| Rivaroxaban alone | 9,117 | Rivaroxaban 5 mg BID |
| ASA alone | 9,126 | ASA 100 mg daily |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of CV death, stroke, or MI**

### Principal Safety Endpoint
**Modified ISTH major bleeding**

### Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause mortality
- Ischemic stroke
- MI
- Limb events (PAD subgroup)

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint: Rivaroxaban + ASA vs ASA Alone

| Outcome | Riva + ASA | ASA Alone | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|------------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **CV death, stroke, MI** | **4.1%** | **5.4%** | **0.76 (0.66-0.86)** | **<0.001** |

**24% relative risk reduction**

### Components

| Outcome | Riva + ASA | ASA Alone | HR |
|---------|------------|-----------|-----|
| CV death | 1.7% | 2.2% | 0.78 |
| Stroke | 0.9% | 1.6% | 0.58 |
| MI | 1.9% | 2.2% | 0.86 |
| **All-cause death** | **3.4%** | **4.1%** | **0.82 (p=0.01)** |

### Bleeding

| Outcome | Riva + ASA | ASA Alone | HR |
|---------|------------|-----------|-----|
| **Major bleeding** | **3.1%** | **1.9%** | **1.70** |
| Fatal bleeding | 0.2% | 0.1% | NS |
| Intracranial bleeding | 0.3% | 0.3% | NS |
| GI bleeding | 1.5% | 0.7% | — |

**More bleeding, but NOT more fatal or intracranial bleeding**

### Net Clinical Benefit

| Outcome | Riva + ASA | ASA Alone |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| CV death, stroke, MI, fatal bleeding, symptomatic bleeding into critical organ | 4.7% | 5.9% |
| **Net benefit** | **Yes** | — |

---

## Rivaroxaban Alone vs ASA Alone

| Outcome | Riva 5 mg BID | ASA Alone | P-value |
|---------|---------------|-----------|---------|
| Primary endpoint | 4.9% | 5.4% | 0.12 (NS) |
| Major bleeding | 2.8% | 1.9% | <0.001 |

**Rivaroxaban alone = not better, more bleeding. The combo is the win.**

---

## PAD Subgroup (COMPASS-PAD)

| Outcome | Riva + ASA | ASA Alone | HR |
|---------|------------|-----------|-----|
| CV death, stroke, MI | 5.1% | 6.9% | 0.72 |
| **Acute limb ischemia** | **0.8%** | **1.5%** | **0.56** |
| **Major amputation** | **0.4%** | **0.8%** | **0.46** |

**Dramatic limb protection — 46% reduction in major amputation**

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **New prevention strategy** — low-dose anticoagulation + aspirin
2. **All-cause mortality benefit** — 18% reduction (rare for secondary prevention)
3. **Stroke prevention** — 42% reduction (best effect)
4. **PAD patients benefit most** — limb protection
5. **Guidelines updated** — ESC Class IIa for stable ASCVD

### FDA Approval
- Rivaroxaban 2.5 mg BID + ASA approved for CAD/PAD (2018)
- Specific indication for high-risk stable ASCVD

### Current Guidelines

| Society | Recommendation | Class |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| ESC | Riva 2.5 BID + ASA in stable CAD/PAD without high bleed risk | IIa |
| AHA/ACC | Consider in stable ASCVD | IIa |
| TASC | Recommended for PAD | IIa |

---

## Who Should Get COMPASS Regimen?

### Ideal Candidates
- Stable CAD + high-risk features (diabetes, CKD, multivessel, PAD)
- PAD patients (greatest benefit)
- Those tolerating ASA without bleeding

### Avoid If
- High bleeding risk (HAS-BLED ≥3)
- Requiring other anticoagulation (AF, mechanical valve)
- Severe CKD (CrCl <15)
- Prior intracranial hemorrhage

---

## Limitations

1. **Three-arm design** — less power for each comparison
2. **GI bleeding increase** — clinically meaningful (1.5% vs 0.7%)
3. **Cost** — rivaroxaban is expensive
4. **Compliance** — BID dosing may be challenging
5. **Specific dose** — only 2.5 mg BID studied (not 5, 10, 20)

---

## COMPASS vs PEGASUS-TIMI 54

| Trial | Drug | Population | Primary HR | Mortality |
|-------|------|------------|------------|-----------|
| **COMPASS** | Riva 2.5 BID + ASA | Stable CAD/PAD | **0.76** | **0.82 (p=0.01)** |
| PEGASUS | Ticagrelor 60 BID + ASA | Post-MI (1-3y) | 0.84 | NS |

**COMPASS showed mortality benefit; PEGASUS did not**

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **2.5 mg BID is the dose** — not vascular dose of rivaroxaban for other indications
2. **Mortality benefit** — rare for antithrombotic therapy
3. **Stroke reduction is strongest** — 42%
4. **PAD patients benefit most** — limb protection
5. **GI bleeding is real** — PPI co-prescription reasonable

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> COMPASS showed that **adding low-dose rivaroxaban to aspirin beats aspirin alone** in stable ASCVD — with mortality benefit. The trade-off is more GI bleeding (but not more fatal bleeding). **Best candidates:** PAD patients (limb protection), high-risk CAD, and those not already on anticoagulation. The 2.5 mg BID dose + aspirin is now guideline-supported for selected stable ASCVD patients.
