# NOTION

**Nordic Aortic Valve Intervention Trial**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2015 (primary), 2021 (10-year) |
| **Journal** | NEJM, Circulation |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1500491 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01057173 |
| **Bottom Line** | First RCT showing TAVR non-inferior to SAVR in low-risk — the pioneer trial |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial (non-inferiority) |
| **N** | 280 |
| **Centers** | 3 Nordic centers (Denmark, Sweden) |
| **Population** | Severe aortic stenosis, ≥70 years, low surgical risk |
| **Mean Age** | 79 years |
| **Mean STS Score** | 3.0% (calculated, not enrollment criterion) |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Significance
- **First low-risk TAVR trial** — paved the way for PARTNER 3 and Evolut Low Risk
- **Longest follow-up** — now 10-year data available
- **Real-world "all-comers"** approach

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| TAVR | 145 | CoreValve (self-expanding, first-generation) |
| SAVR | 135 | Conventional surgical AVR |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of all-cause death, stroke, or MI at 1 year**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- Individual components
- Rehospitalization
- Pacemaker
- Valve performance (gradients, regurgitation)
- Quality of life

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (1 Year)

| Outcome | TAVR | SAVR | Difference | P-value |
|---------|------|------|------------|---------|
| **Death, stroke, or MI** | **13.1%** | **16.3%** | **-3.2%** | **Non-inferior** |

### Component Analysis (1 Year)

| Outcome | TAVR | SAVR |
|---------|------|------|
| All-cause death | 4.9% | 7.5% |
| Stroke | 2.9% | 4.6% |
| MI | 5.7% | 4.8% |

### 30-Day Outcomes

| Outcome | TAVR | SAVR |
|---------|------|------|
| Death | 2.1% | 3.7% |
| Stroke | 1.4% | 3.0% |
| **New pacemaker** | **34.1%** | **1.6%** |
| Major bleeding | 11.3% | 19.3% |
| AKI | 0.7% | 3.1% |
| New A-fib | 16.3% | 57.8% |

---

## Long-Term Follow-Up

### 5-Year Results

| Outcome | TAVR | SAVR | P-value |
|---------|------|------|---------|
| All-cause death | 27.6% | 27.4% | NS |
| Stroke | 6.1% | 8.4% | NS |
| MI | 9.3% | 8.6% | NS |
| Pacemaker | 41.0% | 5.3% | <0.001 |
| Structural valve deterioration | 4.8% | 0% | NS |

### 10-Year Results (Landmark Data)

| Outcome | TAVR | SAVR | P-value |
|---------|------|------|---------|
| All-cause death | 67.3% | 69.9% | NS |
| CV death | 41.8% | 47.0% | NS |
| Stroke | 13.0% | 12.3% | NS |
| MI | 11.9% | 14.4% | NS |
| Bioprosthetic valve failure | 6.7% | 5.7% | NS |
| Reintervention | 1.7% | 5.0% | NS |

**No difference in mortality or valve failure at 10 years**

---

## The Pacemaker Story

### First-Generation CoreValve Problem

| Timepoint | TAVR | SAVR |
|-----------|------|------|
| 30 days | 34.1% | 1.6% |
| 5 years | 41.0% | 5.3% |
| 10 years | ~43% | ~8% |

### Why So High?
- First-generation CoreValve design
- Deep implantation technique (learning curve)
- No cusp overlap technique
- Less emphasis on conduction preservation

### Modern Context
- Evolut Low Risk: 17.4% (improved but still high)
- SAPIEN 3: 6.6%
- Technique refinements have helped

---

## Valve Durability (The Real Question)

### 10-Year Bioprosthetic Valve Failure

| Definition | TAVR | SAVR | P-value |
|------------|------|------|---------|
| SVD (structural) | 6.7% | 5.7% | NS |
| Need for reintervention | 1.7% | 5.0% | NS |

### What This Means
- **No excess valve deterioration with TAVR at 10 years**
- First-generation valve, most rigorous long-term data
- Reassuring for durability concerns

---

## Clinical Impact

### What NOTION Established
1. **Proof of concept** — TAVR could work in low-risk
2. **Paved the way** for PARTNER 3 and Evolut Low Risk
3. **Longest follow-up** — 10-year durability data
4. **All-comers design** — real-world applicability

### What It Couldn't Do
- Small sample size (N=280)
- Single valve platform
- High pacemaker rate limited enthusiasm
- Superseded by larger trials

---

## NOTION vs Pivotal Low-Risk Trials

| Parameter | NOTION | PARTNER 3 | Evolut Low Risk |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| N | 280 | 1,000 | 1,468 |
| Mean age | 79 | 73 | 74 |
| Mean STS | 3.0% | 1.9% | 1.9% |
| Valve | CoreValve | SAPIEN 3 | Evolut R/PRO |
| Pacemaker | 34.1% | 6.6% | 17.4% |
| Result | Non-inferior | Superior | Non-inferior |
| Max follow-up | 10 years | 5 years | 4 years |

---

## Limitations

1. **Small sample size** — 280 patients; underpowered
2. **First-generation device** — CoreValve; high pacemaker
3. **Single region** — Nordic countries only
4. **All-comers** — not strictly low-risk by STS
5. **Superseded** — larger trials with newer devices available

---

## Controversies

### The Pacemaker Problem
- 34% pacemaker rate was unacceptable
- Argued against TAVR expansion
- Modern devices have improved but not eliminated issue

### Sample Size Critics
- N=280 too small for definitive conclusions
- Non-inferiority margin questionable
- Confidence intervals wide

### Historical Value
- Important as first trial, but superseded
- 10-year data uniquely valuable
- Proof of concept vs practice-changing

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **First low-risk RCT** — NOTION paved the way for everything
2. **10-year durability** — 6.7% SVD, comparable to surgery
3. **Pacemaker rates improved** — 34% (CoreValve) → 17% (Evolut) → 6% (SAPIEN)
4. **Small but influential** — N=280 changed the field
5. **Nordic pragmatism** — all-comers, real-world approach

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> NOTION was the **pioneer trial** that proved TAVR could work in low-risk patients and has provided the **longest follow-up data** (10 years) for TAVR durability. The 34% pacemaker rate with first-generation CoreValve was concerning, but the **10-year mortality and valve durability are equivalent to surgery**. While superseded by PARTNER 3 and Evolut Low Risk for practice guidance, NOTION answers the durability question that those trials can't yet address.

> **The lesson:** TAVR valves last. The 10-year data shows no excess structural valve deterioration compared to surgical valves. This addresses the biggest concern about using TAVR in younger, low-risk patients.
