# COMPLETE

**Complete Revascularization with Multivessel PCI for Myocardial Infarction**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2019 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1907775 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01740479 |
| **Bottom Line** | Complete revascularization superior to culprit-only PCI in STEMI with multivessel disease |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 4,041 |
| **Centers** | 140 sites, 31 countries |
| **Population** | STEMI with successful culprit PCI + ≥1 non-culprit lesion ≥70% (or 50-69% with FFR ≤0.80) |
| **Mean Age** | 62 years |
| **Median Follow-up** | 3 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Complete revasc | 2,016 | Culprit PCI + staged non-culprit PCI (during index hospitalization or within 45 days) |
| Culprit-only | 2,025 | Culprit PCI only (non-culprit lesions left alone) |

---

## Endpoints

### Co-Primary Endpoints
1. **CV death or MI**
2. **CV death, MI, or ischemia-driven revascularization**

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoints

| Outcome | Complete | Culprit-Only | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|----------|--------------|-------------|---------|
| **CV death or MI** | **7.8%** | **10.5%** | **0.74 (0.60-0.91)** | **0.004** |
| **CV death, MI, or revasc** | **8.9%** | **16.7%** | **0.51 (0.43-0.61)** | **<0.001** |

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | Complete | Culprit-Only | HR |
|---------|----------|--------------|-----|
| CV death | 2.9% | 3.2% | 0.93 (NS) |
| MI | 5.4% | 8.2% | 0.68 (p=0.002) |
| Ischemia-driven revasc | 1.9% | 7.6% | 0.25 (p<0.001) |

### NNT Calculations

| Outcome | ARR | NNT |
|---------|-----|-----|
| CV death or MI | 2.7% | 37 |
| CV death, MI, or revasc | 7.8% | 13 |

---

## Timing of Non-Culprit PCI

| Approach | % of Complete Arm |
|----------|-------------------|
| During index hospitalization | 80% |
| After discharge (within 45 days) | 20% |

**Both timing strategies showed benefit**

### FFR Utilization
- Only 4% of non-culprit lesions assessed with FFR
- Most treated based on angiography (≥70% stenosis)
- Suggests angiographic guidance is acceptable in this setting

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **Standard of care shift** — complete revascularization now preferred in STEMI + MVD
2. **Guidelines updated** — Class IIa → Class I for staged complete revasc
3. **Practical timing** — can do during index hospitalization or within 45 days
4. **MI reduction** — hard endpoint benefit (not just revascularization)

### Current Guidelines (2023)

| Society | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| ACC/AHA | Class IIa: Complete revasc reasonable in STEMI + MVD |
| ESC | Class I: Routine revascularization of non-culprit lesions should be considered |

---

## Comparison: COMPLETE vs CULPRIT-SHOCK

| Factor | COMPLETE | CULPRIT-SHOCK |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Population | STEMI, no shock | STEMI + cardiogenic shock |
| N | 4,041 | 706 |
| Complete revasc result | **Beneficial** | **Harmful** |
| 30-day death/RRT | — | 45.9% (complete) vs 34.9% (culprit-only) |
| Interpretation | Stable STEMI → complete | Shock → culprit-only |

**Critical distinction: Cardiogenic shock is different — culprit-only is safer**

---

## Limitations

1. **Open-label** — patients knew allocation
2. **Low FFR use** — 4%; unclear if FFR-negative lesions needed treatment
3. **Excluded cardiogenic shock** — CULPRIT-SHOCK applies to shock
4. **Timing flexibility** — optimal timing unclear (index vs staged)
5. **Non-culprit definition** — ≥70% visual, may include insignificant lesions

---

## Controversies

### FFR for Non-Culprit Lesions?
- COMPLETE used mostly angiography
- FLOWER-MI (2021): FFR-guided non-culprit PCI was non-inferior to angio-guided
- Current practice: Either approach acceptable

### Timing: Index vs Staged?
- 80% done during index hospitalization in COMPLETE
- BIOVASC (2023): Index complete revasc safe
- MULTISTARS-AMI (2023): Immediate complete revasc safe
- **Consensus:** Both approaches work; do it before discharge if feasible

### Which Lesions to Treat?
- COMPLETE used ≥70% visual or FFR ≤0.80 for 50-69%
- Some treated lesions may have been FFR-negative
- Counter: Trial was positive, so approach works

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **STEMI + MVD = complete revascularization** — that's the COMPLETE message
2. **Cardiogenic shock is the exception** — use CULPRIT-SHOCK data (culprit-only)
3. **Timing is flexible** — index or within 45 days both acceptable
4. **MI prevention is real** — not just revascularization reduction
5. **FFR optional** — angiographic guidance works in this setting

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> COMPLETE settled the debate: **in stable STEMI patients with multivessel disease, fix all significant lesions — not just the culprit.** The benefit is driven by MI reduction, a hard endpoint. Do it during the index hospitalization or within 45 days. **Exception: cardiogenic shock → culprit-only (per CULPRIT-SHOCK).**
