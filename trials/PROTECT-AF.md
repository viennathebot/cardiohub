# PROTECT-AF

**Watchman Left Atrial Appendage System for Embolic Protection in Patients with Atrial Fibrillation**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2009 (initial), 2014 (5-year) |
| **Journal** | Lancet (initial), JAMA (5-year) |
| **DOI** | 10.1016/S0140-6736(09)61343-X |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT00129545 |
| **Bottom Line** | LAAO non-inferior to warfarin for stroke prevention in AF; better long-term outcomes |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 707 |
| **Centers** | 59 US sites |
| **Population** | Non-valvular AF with CHADS₂ ≥1, eligible for warfarin |
| **Mean Age** | 72 years |
| **Mean CHADS₂** | 2.2 |
| **Randomization** | 2:1 (Watchman : Warfarin) |

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Watchman | 463 | LAAO + warfarin 45 days + ASA/clopidogrel, then ASA alone |
| Warfarin | 244 | Long-term warfarin (INR 2-3) |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Efficacy Endpoint
**Composite of stroke, systemic embolism, and cardiovascular/unexplained death**

### Primary Safety Endpoint
**Procedure-related complications (pericardial effusion, stroke, device embolization)**

---

## Results

### Primary Efficacy (Mean 18 Months Follow-up)

| Outcome | Watchman | Warfarin | RR (95% CI) | P (non-inferiority) |
|---------|----------|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Primary composite** | **3.0/100 pt-yrs** | **4.9/100 pt-yrs** | **0.62 (0.35-1.25)** | **0.004 (met)** |

### Safety Events (Periprocedural)

| Event | Watchman |
|-------|----------|
| Serious pericardial effusion | 4.8% |
| Procedure-related stroke | 1.1% |
| Device embolization | 0.6% |

**Learning curve:** Safety improved significantly over trial duration

### 5-Year Follow-up (JAMA 2014)

| Outcome | Watchman | Warfarin | RR | P-value |
|---------|----------|----------|-----|---------|
| **Primary composite** | **2.3/100 pt-yrs** | **3.8/100 pt-yrs** | **0.60** | **NI met** |
| All-cause mortality | 3.2/100 pt-yrs | 4.8/100 pt-yrs | 0.66 | 0.04 |
| CV mortality | 1.0/100 pt-yrs | 2.4/100 pt-yrs | 0.40 | 0.005 |
| Hemorrhagic stroke | 0.2/100 pt-yrs | 1.1/100 pt-yrs | 0.15 | 0.004 |

### The Crossover Issue
- 34% of Watchman patients had some warfarin use post-45 days (failed discontinuation)
- 23% of warfarin patients discontinued at some point
- ITT analysis still showed benefit

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **FDA approval** of Watchman (March 2015) — after PREVAIL confirmatory data
2. **Alternative to anticoagulation** for high-bleeding-risk AF patients
3. **Structural heart expansion** — established LAAO as procedure category
4. **New patient conversations** — "there's a device option"

### PREVAIL: Confirmatory Trial

| Parameter | PROTECT-AF | PREVAIL |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| N | 707 | 407 |
| CHADS₂ | 2.2 | 2.6 |
| Procedure success | 91% | 95% |
| Safety events | 8.7% | 4.2% |
| Non-inferiority | Met | Met (one of two endpoints) |

**PREVAIL showed improved safety with operator experience**

---

## Long-Term Data

### 5-Year Pooled Analysis (PROTECT-AF + PREVAIL)

| Outcome | Watchman | Warfarin | HR | P-value |
|---------|----------|----------|-----|---------|
| All-cause mortality | 12.3/100 pt-yrs | 14.7/100 pt-yrs | 0.73 | 0.035 |
| Hemorrhagic stroke | 0.2/100 pt-yrs | 0.9/100 pt-yrs | 0.20 | <0.001 |
| Ischemic stroke | Similar | Similar | NS | — |

**Key finding:** Mortality benefit driven by reduction in hemorrhagic stroke and non-stroke bleeding

---

## Limitations

1. **Open-label** — no sham control, potential bias
2. **Warfarin comparator** — not DOACs (outdated control)
3. **45-day warfarin bridge** — still requires anticoagulation initially
4. **Single device** — only Watchman studied
5. **Learning curve** — early safety events high

---

## Controversies

### DOAC Era Relevance
- Critics: "Would Watchman beat apixaban?"
- PRAGUE-17 (2020): Watchman vs DOACs — non-inferior
- But DOAC use is simpler, no procedure
- Counter: Watchman allows true OAC discontinuation

### High-Risk Exclusion
- Trial excluded highest-bleeding-risk patients
- These are the patients who might benefit most
- Registries (EWOLUTION, etc.) show safety in high-risk

### Cost-Effectiveness
- Device + procedure = $30,000+
- Long-term medication cost offset
- Cost-effective if patient lives >2-3 years

## Clinical Pearls

1. **LAA anatomy matters** — chicken wing vs cauliflower affects success
2. **TEE follow-up essential** — confirm closure, rule out thrombus
3. **Post-procedure protocol varies** — DAPT vs warfarin bridge
4. **Operator experience critical** — safety improved dramatically with volume
5. **5-year data > 1-year** — mortality benefit emerged late

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> PROTECT-AF established LAAO as a legitimate stroke prevention strategy for AF. The 5-year data showing **mortality benefit and dramatic hemorrhagic stroke reduction** solidified its role. In the DOAC era, Watchman is primarily for patients who can't tolerate long-term anticoagulation — a smaller but important population.

> **The real-world niche:** HAS-BLED ≥3, prior ICH, fall risk, or patient preference against lifelong medication.
