# CHAMPION PHOENIX

**Cangrelor versus Standard Therapy to Achieve Optimal Management of Platelet Inhibition**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2013 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1310938 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01156610 |
| **Bottom Line** | IV cangrelor superior to clopidogrel loading in reducing PCI-related ischemic events |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled |
| **N** | 11,145 |
| **Centers** | 153 sites, international |
| **Population** | Patients undergoing PCI (urgent or elective) |
| **Mean Age** | 64 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### What is Cangrelor?
- **IV P2Y12 inhibitor** — only one available
- **Immediate onset** — full inhibition within 2 minutes
- **Short half-life** — ~5 minutes
- **Rapid offset** — platelet function normalizes in 60 minutes

### Why Cangrelor Matters
- Oral P2Y12 inhibitors take 2-4 hours for effect
- Many patients need immediate platelet inhibition (STEMI, urgent PCI)
- Bridge to surgery (reversible)

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Cangrelor | 5,472 | IV cangrelor + placebo clopidogrel at PCI, then clopidogrel post |
| Control | 5,529 | IV placebo + clopidogrel 600mg or 300mg loading at PCI |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of death, MI, ischemia-driven revascularization, or stent thrombosis at 48 hours**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- Stent thrombosis at 48 hours
- Death at 48 hours
- TIMI major bleeding
- Components of primary endpoint

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (48 Hours)

| Outcome | Cangrelor | Clopidogrel | OR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Primary composite** | **4.7%** | **5.9%** | **0.78 (0.66-0.93)** | **0.005** |

**ARR = 1.2%** | **NNT = 84**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | Cangrelor | Clopidogrel | OR | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----|---------|
| Death | 0.3% | 0.3% | NS | NS |
| MI | 3.8% | 4.7% | 0.80 | 0.02 |
| IDR | 0.5% | 0.6% | NS | NS |
| **Stent thrombosis** | **0.8%** | **1.4%** | **0.62** | **0.01** |

### Stent Thrombosis Reduction

| Timepoint | Cangrelor | Clopidogrel | Reduction |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| 48 hours | 0.8% | 1.4% | 38% |
| Intra-procedural | 0.2% | 0.5% | 60% |

**Cangrelor cuts stent thrombosis by 38%**

## Failed Predecessors: CHAMPION Trials

### Why Previous Trials Failed

| Trial | Issue | Result |
|-------|-------|--------|
| CHAMPION PCI | Transition to clopidogrel problematic | Negative |
| CHAMPION PLATFORM | Underpowered | Negative |
| **CHAMPION PHOENIX** | Fixed transition protocol | **Positive** |

### The Transition Problem
- Early trials: Clopidogrel given immediately after cangrelor stopped
- Drug-drug interaction: Clopidogrel inactive metabolite blocked by cangrelor
- Solution: Give clopidogrel while cangrelor still running (competitive binding)

## Pharmacology: Why Cangrelor Works

### Mechanism Comparison

| Property | Cangrelor | Clopidogrel | Ticagrelor |
|----------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Administration | IV | Oral | Oral |
| Onset | 2 minutes | 2-4 hours | 30-60 min |
| Half-life | 5 minutes | Active metabolite ~30 min | ~12 hours |
| Reversibility | Minutes | Days | 24-36 hours |
| Prodrug | No | Yes | No |

### Clinical Implications
- **STEMI:** Immediate inhibition when door-to-balloon time matters
- **Uncertain anatomy:** Can stop if surgery needed
- **NPO patients:** IV when can't take oral

## CHAMPION PHOENIX vs ACCOAST

### Comparison with Prasugrel Pretreatment

| Trial | Question | Result |
|-------|----------|--------|
| CHAMPION PHOENIX | Cangrelor at PCI vs clopidogrel load | Cangrelor better |
| ACCOAST | Prasugrel pre-PCI vs at-PCI | No benefit, more bleeding |

**Lesson:** The value is in immediate inhibition AT TIME OF PCI, not hours before.

## Limitations

1. **Clopidogrel comparator** — not ticagrelor or prasugrel
2. **48-hour endpoint** — short-term benefit; long-term similar
3. **Cost** — ~$1,000/dose limits use
4. **Modest NNT** — 84 patients to prevent one event
5. **Industry-sponsored** — The Medicines Company funded

---

## Controversies

### Clopidogrel Comparator
- Would cangrelor beat ticagrelor/prasugrel loading?
- CHAMPION PHOENIX only tested against clopidogrel
- Post-hoc analyses suggest benefit maintained but weaker

### Cost-Effectiveness
- $1,000/dose vs pennies for clopidogrel
- NNT = 84 makes cost per event prevented high
- Most benefit in highest-risk patients

### Who Really Needs It?
- STEMI without pretreatment — clear indication
- Elective PCI with time for oral loading — less clear
- "Bridge to surgery" — useful but how common?

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **2-minute onset** — full platelet inhibition immediately
2. **5-minute half-life** — surgery-safe within 60 minutes
3. **Stent thrombosis -38%** — the headline benefit
4. **NNT = 84** — modest but real for ischemic events
5. **Transition matters** — give oral P2Y12 while cangrelor running (not after)

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> CHAMPION PHOENIX proved that **immediate P2Y12 inhibition at the time of PCI** reduces ischemic events, especially stent thrombosis. Cangrelor's value is its **pharmacokinetics**: 2-minute onset, 5-minute offset. Use it when you need **immediate and reversible** platelet inhibition — STEMI without pretreatment, uncertain need for surgery, or NPO patients. The NNT of 84 is modest, so reserve for **highest-risk situations** rather than routine elective PCI.

> **The conversation:** "This IV blood thinner works in 2 minutes and wears off in an hour. We use it when we need fast, controllable platelet inhibition — like when someone's having a heart attack and we can't wait for pills to kick in."
