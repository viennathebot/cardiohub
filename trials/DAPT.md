# DAPT

**Dual Antiplatelet Therapy Study**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2014 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1409312 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT00977938 |
| **Bottom Line** | Extended DAPT (30 months) reduces MI and stent thrombosis but increases bleeding vs 12 months |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled |
| **N** | 9,961 (from 11,648 initial enrollees) |
| **Centers** | 452 sites, 11 countries |
| **Population** | DES patients event-free at 12 months |
| **Mean Age** | 61 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Design Feature
- **Two-phase study:**
  - All patients got DAPT for 12 months
  - If event-free at 12 months → randomized to continue or stop thienopyridine
- **Thienopyridine:** 65% clopidogrel, 35% prasugrel

### Arms (Post-12 Month Randomization)

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Extended DAPT | 4,972 | Continue thienopyridine 12-30 months |
| Placebo | 4,989 | Aspirin + placebo 12-30 months |

---

## Endpoints

### Co-Primary Efficacy Endpoints
1. **Stent thrombosis** (12-30 months)
2. **MACCE** (death, MI, stroke)

### Primary Safety Endpoint
**Moderate or severe bleeding (GUSTO)**

---

## Results

### Stent Thrombosis

| Outcome | Extended DAPT | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|---------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Definite/probable ST** | **0.4%** | **1.4%** | **0.29 (0.17-0.48)** | **<0.001** |

**71% relative reduction in stent thrombosis**

### MACCE and Components

| Outcome | Extended DAPT | Placebo | HR | P-value |
|---------|---------------|---------|-----|---------|
| **MACCE** | **4.3%** | **5.9%** | **0.71** | **<0.001** |
| MI | 2.1% | 4.1% | 0.47 | <0.001 |
| Stroke | 0.8% | 0.6% | NS | — |
| Death (all-cause) | 2.0% | 1.5% | 1.36 | 0.05 |

### Bleeding

| Outcome | Extended DAPT | Placebo | P-value |
|---------|---------------|---------|---------|
| **Mod/severe bleeding** | **2.5%** | **1.6%** | **0.001** |
| GUSTO severe | 0.8% | 0.4% | 0.03 |

---

## The Mortality Signal

### Concerning Finding
| Outcome | Extended DAPT | Placebo | HR | P-value |
|---------|---------------|---------|-----|---------|
| All-cause death | 2.0% | 1.5% | 1.36 | 0.05 |
| Non-CV death | 1.0% | 0.5% | 2.23 | 0.002 |

**More deaths with extended DAPT — driven by non-CV (bleeding, cancer)**

### Interpretation
- Stent thrombosis/MI down, but bleeding-related deaths up
- Net clinical benefit unclear
- Patient selection critical

## The DAPT Score

### Derived from Trial Data
Predicts who benefits from extended DAPT vs who is harmed

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Age ≥75 | -2 |
| Age 65-74 | -1 |
| Age <65 | 0 |
| Current smoker | +1 |
| Diabetes | +1 |
| MI at presentation | +1 |
| Prior PCI or MI | +1 |
| Paclitaxel-eluting stent | +1 |
| Stent diameter <3mm | +1 |
| CHF or LVEF <30% | +2 |
| Vein graft PCI | +2 |

### Score Interpretation
| Score | Recommendation |
|-------|----------------|
| ≥2 | Extended DAPT benefit (ischemia >> bleeding) |
| <2 | Standard DAPT (bleeding risk outweighs benefit) |

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **Individualized DAPT duration** — not one-size-fits-all
2. **DAPT score adoption** — tool for decision-making
3. **High-risk patients identified** — who benefits from extension
4. **Bleeding risk recognized** — extended DAPT has costs
5. **Trade-off conversations** — shared decision-making

### Guideline Recommendations

| Situation | ACC/AHA Recommendation |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Low bleed risk + high thrombotic risk | Extended DAPT reasonable (IIb) |
| High bleed risk | Shorter DAPT (1-3 months) |
| DAPT score ≥2 | Consider extension |
| DAPT score <2 | Standard 12 months |

---

## DAPT in Context: Evolution of Duration

| Era | Default Duration | Evidence |
|-----|------------------|----------|
| BMS (1990s) | 1 month | Historical |
| Early DES (2000s) | 12 months | Expert opinion |
| **DAPT trial (2014)** | **12 vs 30 months** | **RCT data** |
| Short DAPT (2019+) | 1-3 months → P2Y12 mono | TWILIGHT, TICO, STOPDAPT-2 |

**The field has shifted toward SHORTER DAPT, not longer**

---

## Limitations

1. **Event-free selection** — only patients without events at 12 months randomized
2. **Old stents** — 43% first-generation DES (higher thrombosis rates)
3. **Mortality signal** — unexplained excess non-CV deaths
4. **2014 context** — current DES have lower thrombosis rates
5. **Doesn't reflect de-escalation era** — TWILIGHT approach now preferred

---

## Controversies

### Is Extended DAPT Still Relevant?
- Critics: Modern DES have low thrombosis; extended DAPT unnecessary
- Counter: High-risk subset (DAPT score ≥2) still benefits
- Reality: Most patients now get short DAPT + P2Y12 mono (TWILIGHT)

### The Death Signal
- Unexplained non-CV mortality increase
- Possibly chance finding (multiple comparisons)
- But concerning enough to mandate shared decision-making

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Calculate the DAPT score** — it predicts benefit vs harm
2. **Score ≥2 = consider extension** — MI/ST reduction outweighs bleeding
3. **Score <2 = don't extend** — bleeding risk too high
4. **Modern practice favors shorter** — TWILIGHT approach
5. **Bleeding kills too** — the mortality signal is a reminder

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> DAPT showed that **extended dual antiplatelet therapy is a trade-off, not a free lunch.** Yes, MI and stent thrombosis go down — but bleeding goes up, and there was a mortality signal. Use the **DAPT score** to identify the ~20% of patients who benefit from extension. For most patients today, the trend is toward **shorter DAPT + P2Y12 monotherapy** (TWILIGHT, TICO).

> **The question has flipped:** It's no longer "should we extend DAPT?" but "can we safely shorten it?"
