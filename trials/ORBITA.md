# ORBITA

**Objective Randomised Blinded Investigation with Optimal Medical Therapy of Angioplasty in Stable Angina**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2018 |
| **Journal** | Lancet |
| **DOI** | 10.1016/S0140-6736(17)32714-9 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT02062593 |
| **Bottom Line** | PCI did NOT improve exercise time vs sham procedure in stable angina — challenged the placebo effect |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, sham-controlled |
| **N** | 200 |
| **Centers** | 5 UK sites |
| **Population** | Stable angina, single-vessel CAD (≥70% stenosis), ischemia on stress imaging |
| **Mean Age** | 66 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Revolutionary Design Feature
- **SHAM CONTROL** — both groups underwent cath; PCI arm got stent, sham arm got no intervention
- Patients blinded to allocation
- First sham-controlled PCI trial for angina

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| PCI | 105 | Angioplasty + DES |
| Sham | 95 | Angiography only (no intervention) |

### Pre-Randomization Phase
- All patients optimized on medical therapy for 6 weeks
- Stress echo and FFR performed
- Only then randomized

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Change in exercise time (treadmill) at 6 weeks**

### Secondary Endpoints
- Peak oxygen uptake (VO₂)
- Angina frequency (Seattle Angina Questionnaire)
- Freedom from angina
- Duke Treadmill Score

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint: Exercise Time

| Outcome | PCI | Sham | Difference | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|------------|---------|
| **Exercise time increase** | **28.4 sec** | **11.8 sec** | **+16.6 sec** | **0.20** |

**No significant difference**

### Secondary Endpoints

| Outcome | PCI | Sham | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|---------|
| Peak VO₂ | +0.6 mL/kg/min | +0.4 mL/kg/min | NS |
| Angina freedom | 49% | 32% | 0.02 |
| SAQ angina frequency | +10.6 | +2.9 | 0.01 |
| Duke Treadmill Score | +1.4 | +0.5 | NS |

### FFR Subgroup
| FFR | PCI | Sham | Interaction |
|-----|-----|------|-------------|
| FFR ≤0.80 | +29 sec | -3 sec | Trend favoring PCI |
| FFR >0.80 | +29 sec | +35 sec | No difference |

---

## The Controversy

### What ORBITA Showed
1. **Placebo effect is real** — sham patients improved too
2. **Objective measures didn't differ** — exercise time, peak VO₂
3. **Subjective measures did differ** — angina scores favored PCI
4. **FFR-positive subset** — possible real benefit (underpowered)

### The Criticism
1. **Small sample size** — 200 patients, underpowered
2. **Short follow-up** — 6 weeks only
3. **Single-vessel disease** — not representative
4. **6 weeks of optimization first** — many already improved
5. **Exercise time not patient-centered** — who cares about 16 seconds?

### The Defense
- First rigorous placebo-controlled PCI trial
- Showed need for better evidence
- Led to ORBITA-2

---

## ORBITA-2 (2023)

### Design Changes
- N = 301
- **No antianginal medications** — cleaner test of PCI effect
- Primary: angina symptom score (patient-reported)

### Results

| Outcome | PCI | Sham | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|---------|
| **Angina symptom score** | **2.9** | **5.6** | **<0.001** |
| Angina-free at 12 weeks | 69% | 35% | <0.001 |

**ORBITA-2: PCI DID improve symptoms when meds removed**

---

## Clinical Impact

### What We Learned
1. **OMT is powerful** — optimized medical therapy alone helps many
2. **Placebo effect in interventional cardiology** — documented for first time
3. **Symptoms are real, but so is placebo** — need to separate
4. **FFR-positive lesions may matter more** — trend in ORBITA, proven in FAME 2

### Practice Changes
- More thoughtful about PCI for stable angina
- OMT optimization first
- FFR to confirm ischemia
- Honest conversations about placebo effect

---

## Reconciling ORBITA with Other Trials

| Trial | Sham? | Primary Endpoint | Result |
|-------|-------|------------------|--------|
| ORBITA | Yes | Exercise time | NS |
| ORBITA-2 | Yes | Angina score (no meds) | PCI better |
| FAME 2 | No | MACE | PCI better |
| ISCHEMIA | No | MACE | NS (but angina better) |

**Synthesis:** PCI doesn't prevent events in stable CAD (ISCHEMIA) but does relieve symptoms (ORBITA-2), especially when optimized meds aren't enough.

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Optimize meds first** — many patients improve without PCI
2. **Placebo effect is real** — ~30% of angina improvement may be non-specific
3. **FFR-positive lesions** — more likely to have real benefit
4. **Symptoms matter** — ORBITA-2 showed PCI helps angina
5. **Use for shared decision-making** — "PCI helps symptoms, but so does medical therapy"

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> ORBITA was a wake-up call: **much of what we attributed to PCI benefit may have been placebo effect.** But ORBITA-2 clarified: when you remove antianginal medications, **PCI does relieve angina better than sham.** The message isn't "PCI doesn't work" — it's "optimize meds first, confirm ischemia with FFR, and have honest conversations about what PCI can and can't do."
