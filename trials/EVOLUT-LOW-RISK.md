# EVOLUT Low Risk

**Self-Expanding TAVR in Low-Risk Patients**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2019 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1816885 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT02701283 |
| **Bottom Line** | Self-expanding TAVR (Evolut) non-inferior to SAVR in low-risk severe AS |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial (non-inferiority) |
| **N** | 1,468 (analyzed: 1,403) |
| **Centers** | 86 sites (US, Canada, Australia, Japan, Europe) |
| **Population** | Severe aortic stenosis, low surgical risk |
| **Mean Age** | 74 years |
| **Mean STS Score** | 1.9% |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |
| **Non-inferiority margin** | 6% |

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| TAVR | 734 | Evolut R or Evolut PRO (self-expanding) |
| SAVR | 734 | Conventional surgical AVR |

### Key Differences from PARTNER 3
| Factor | Evolut Low Risk | PARTNER 3 |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| Valve type | Self-expanding | Balloon-expandable |
| Trial design | Non-inferiority | Superiority |
| Primary endpoint | 24 months | 12 months |
| Device | Evolut R/PRO | SAPIEN 3 |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of all-cause death or disabling stroke at 24 months** (Bayesian analysis)

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause mortality
- Disabling and non-disabling stroke
- Rehospitalization
- New pacemaker
- Aortic regurgitation
- Mean gradient

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (24 Months)

| Outcome | TAVR | SAVR | Difference | Posterior Probability |
|---------|------|------|------------|----------------------|
| **Death or disabling stroke** | **5.3%** | **6.7%** | **-1.4%** | **>99.9% non-inferior** |

**Non-inferiority achieved with >99.9% probability**

|---------|------|------|---------|

## The Pacemaker Issue

### Rates Comparison

| Trial | TAVR Device | Pacemaker Rate |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Evolut Low Risk** | Self-expanding | **17.4%** |
| PARTNER 3 | Balloon-expandable | 6.6% |
| SAVR (both trials) | Surgical | 4-6% |

### Why Self-Expanding Valves Need More Pacemakers
- Deeper implantation in LVOT
- Radial force on conduction system
- Contact with membranous septum
- AV node proximity

### Clinical Implications
- Pre-existing RBBB = higher risk
- Implant depth matters (cusp overlap technique)
- May offset A-fib benefit

## TAVR Low-Risk Landscape

### Head-to-Head Comparison

| Parameter | Evolut Low Risk | PARTNER 3 |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| N | 1,468 | 1,000 |
| Mean STS | 1.9% | 1.9% |
| Primary endpoint | Non-inferiority | Superiority |
| Result | Non-inferior | Superior |
| Death/stroke at 1yr | 2.6% vs 4.2% | 2.2% vs 5.6% |
| Pacemaker | 17.4% vs 6.1% | 6.6% vs 4.1% |
| A-fib | 7.7% vs 35.4% | 7.7% vs 35.4% |

### Why Different Conclusions?
- **Trial design:** Non-inferiority vs superiority
- **Valve platform:** Self-expanding vs balloon-expandable
- **Endpoint timing:** 24 months vs 12 months

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **FDA approval** of Evolut R/PRO for low-risk AS (2019)
2. **Both TAVR platforms approved** for all risk categories
3. **Device choice** becomes anatomy/preference-driven, not risk-driven
4. **Pacemaker counseling** essential with self-expanding valves

### Guideline Updates (2020 ACC/AHA)
- **≥80 years or ≤10-year life expectancy:** TAVR Class I
- **<80 years, good candidate:** TAVR Class IIa
- **Heart Team decision** considering all factors

---

## Choosing Between TAVR Platforms

### Favor Balloon-Expandable (SAPIEN)
- Pre-existing conduction disease (RBBB)
- Small annulus (better sizing precision)
- Horizontal aorta (better positioning)
- Pacemaker avoidance priority

### Favor Self-Expanding (Evolut)
- Bicuspid valve (better expansion)
- Severe calcification (radial force)
- Need for hemodynamic optimization (lower gradients)
- Larger annulus

---

## Limitations

1. **Non-inferiority design** — can't prove superiority
2. **High pacemaker rate** — 17.4% is a real concern
3. **Paravalvular leak higher** — 3.5% moderate
4. **Selected population** — highly screened
5. **Short follow-up** — 24 months primary

---

## Controversies

### Pacemaker Trade-Off
- 17.4% pacemaker rate vs 6.1% with surgery
- Does this offset the procedural benefits?
- RV pacing and long-term HF risk?

### Optimal Device Selection
- Which patients should get which valve?
- Annulus size, anatomy, conduction status
- No head-to-head SAPIEN vs Evolut trial

### Durability Concerns
- 4-year data reassuring
- 10+ year data needed for low-risk patients
- Lifetime management planning

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Non-inferior ≠ equal** — PARTNER 3 showed superiority, Evolut showed non-inferiority
2. **Pacemaker rate 17%** — must discuss with patients; higher than SAPIEN
3. **Lower gradients** — Evolut has hemodynamic advantage (8.6 vs 11.2 mmHg)
4. **A-fib reduction** — both platforms dramatically reduce post-op A-fib
5. **RBBB = caution** — pre-existing conduction disease increases pacemaker risk

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> Evolut Low Risk proved that **self-expanding TAVR is a valid option** for low-risk severe AS, but with a caveat: the **17.4% pacemaker rate** is significantly higher than balloon-expandable TAVR or surgery. For patients where pacemaker avoidance is priority (young, active, no conduction disease), SAPIEN may be preferred. For patients where optimal hemodynamics matter (small annulus, need low gradients), Evolut may be preferred. **It's not about which valve is "better" — it's about which is right for the patient in front of you.**

> **The conversation:** "Both TAVR valves work well for low-risk patients. The self-expanding valve has slightly lower pressure gradients but a higher chance of needing a pacemaker — about 1 in 6 patients. The balloon-expandable valve has a lower pacemaker risk — about 1 in 15. Let's talk about what matters most to you."
