# COURAGE

**Clinical Outcomes Utilizing Revascularization and Aggressive Drug Evaluation**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2007 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa070829 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT00007657 |
| **Bottom Line** | PCI + OMT no better than OMT alone for death/MI in stable CAD — the trial that started the "stable CAD" debate |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 2,287 |
| **Centers** | 50 US and Canadian sites |
| **Population** | Stable CAD with objective evidence of ischemia and significant stenosis |
| **Mean Age** | 62 years |
| **Mean LVEF** | 61% |
| **Median Follow-up** | 4.6 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Inclusion
- Stable angina or asymptomatic with ischemia
- ≥70% stenosis in ≥1 proximal coronary artery
- Evidence of ischemia (stress test positive or recent ACS >2 months)

### Key Exclusion
- ACS within 1 month
- Left main ≥50%
- LVEF <30%
- Refractory angina

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| PCI + OMT | 1,149 | PCI (mostly BMS era) + aggressive medical therapy |
| OMT alone | 1,138 | Aggressive medical therapy alone |

---

## What "Optimal Medical Therapy" Meant

### Medication Targets (Aggressive)

| Target | Goal |
|--------|------|
| LDL cholesterol | <85 mg/dL (many reached <70) |
| Blood pressure | <130/80 mmHg |
| Antiplatelet | Aspirin + clopidogrel in PCI arm |
| Beta-blocker | For all with ischemia |
| ACE inhibitor | For all |
| Smoking cessation | Aggressive |
| Exercise | Cardiac rehab encouraged |

**OMT was truly optimized** — this was aggressive risk factor modification

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of death from any cause or nonfatal MI**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- Hospitalization for ACS
- Freedom from angina
- Quality of life
- Revascularization

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (4.6 Years)

| Outcome | PCI + OMT | OMT Alone | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Death or MI** | **19.0%** | **18.5%** | **1.05 (0.87-1.27)** | **0.62** |

**No difference**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | PCI + OMT | OMT Alone | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Death | 7.6% | 8.3% | NS |
| MI | 13.2% | 12.3% | NS |
| ACS hospitalization | 12.4% | 11.8% | NS |
| Stroke | 2.1% | 1.6% | NS |

### Angina Relief

| Timepoint | PCI + OMT Angina-Free | OMT Alone Angina-Free |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 1 year | 66% | 58% |
| 3 years | 72% | 67% |
| 5 years | 74% | 72% |

**PCI reduced angina early, but converged by 5 years**

### Crossover

| Direction | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| OMT → PCI/CABG | 32.6% (over 5 years) |
| PCI → repeat revasc | 21.1% |

---

## Long-Term Follow-Up

### 15-Year Mortality

| Outcome | PCI + OMT | OMT Alone | P-value |
|---------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| All-cause death | ~38% | ~37% | NS |

**No long-term survival difference**

---

## Clinical Impact

### What COURAGE Changed
1. **Challenged reflex PCI** — seeing stenosis doesn't mean you fix it
2. **OMT is powerful** — intensive medical therapy works
3. **Stable CAD ≠ emergency** — time for conversation
4. **Symptom-driven revascularization** — angina relief, not MI prevention
5. **Foundation for ISCHEMIA** — set up the larger trial

### What COURAGE Did NOT Say
- Does NOT apply to ACS
- Does NOT apply to left main disease
- Does NOT mean PCI doesn't help angina
- Does NOT mean anatomy doesn't matter

## COURAGE vs ISCHEMIA

| Parameter | COURAGE | ISCHEMIA |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Year | 2007 | 2020 |
| N | 2,287 | 5,179 |
| Era | BMS | DES |
| Ischemia proof | Angiography | Stress imaging |
| Left main | Excluded (angio) | Excluded (CCTA) |
| Primary result | Neutral | Neutral |
| Conclusion | Same | Same |

**ISCHEMIA confirmed COURAGE 13 years later with modern stents**

---

## The "COURAGE Effect"

### Before COURAGE
- "Significant stenosis" → "needs PCI"
- Anatomy drove decisions
- Little emphasis on OMT optimization

### After COURAGE
- "What are the symptoms?"
- "Is OMT optimized?"
- "Is this for events or symptoms?"
- Shared decision-making

---

## Limitations

1. **Bare metal stent era** — mostly BMS; DES may differ (but ISCHEMIA showed similar)
2. **Selected population** — excluded unstable, left main, low EF
3. **High crossover** — 32.6% OMT patients got revascularized
4. **No FFR** — anatomic guidance only
5. **Angina only partially captured** — early QoL differences

---

## Controversies

### "But My Stents Are Better Now"
- COURAGE used mostly BMS
- Critics: DES would change results
- ISCHEMIA (2020): Modern DES, same result

### The Crossover Problem
- 1 in 3 OMT patients got PCI eventually
- Does this dilute the difference?
- Counter: ITT analysis is appropriate; crossover happens in real world

### What About FFR?
- COURAGE: Angiographic stenosis ≥70%
- FAME 2: FFR-guided PCI showed benefit
- Reconciliation: FFR identifies different patients than angiography

---

## COURAGE in the Context of Ischemia-Guided Revascularization

| Trial | Selection | Result |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| COURAGE | Angiographic stenosis | Neutral |
| ISCHEMIA | Imaging ischemia | Neutral |
| **FAME 2** | **FFR ≤0.80** | **PCI superior** |

**Synthesis:** Anatomy and imaging ischemia don't predict PCI benefit. FFR-proven ischemia does.

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **OMT is powerful** — LDL <70, BP <130/80, aspirin, statin, ACEi
2. **PCI helps angina** — not survival in stable CAD
3. **32% crossover** — many OMT patients eventually need intervention
4. **Angina converges** — early PCI benefit fades by 5 years
5. **Stable CAD = time to talk** — not emergency

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> COURAGE proved that in **stable CAD**, adding PCI to optimal medical therapy does NOT reduce death or MI. PCI relieves angina faster, but the gap closes over time. This doesn't mean PCI is useless — it means **revascularize for symptoms, not to prevent events**. Always optimize medications first. If symptoms persist despite OMT, PCI is reasonable. If no symptoms, medical therapy alone is safe.

> **The conversation:** "Your blockages are significant, but you're not having a heart attack. Studies show that unless you have chest pain that bothers you, stents don't prevent heart attacks better than aggressive medical treatment. We can try medications first and do stents if your symptoms don't improve. Or, if your symptoms are limiting, stents can help you feel better faster."
