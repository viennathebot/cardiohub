# SYMPLICITY HTN-3

**Renal Denervation in Patients with Uncontrolled Hypertension**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2014 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1402670 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01418261 |
| **Bottom Line** | First sham-controlled RDN trial — failed to meet primary endpoint, set field back 5+ years, but taught us everything about how to do RDN right |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, single-blind, sham-controlled trial |
| **N** | 535 |
| **Centers** | 88 US sites |
| **Population** | Resistant hypertension (SBP ≥160 despite ≥3 meds including diuretic) |
| **Mean Age** | 57 years |
| **Mean SBP** | 180 mmHg |
| **Randomization** | 2:1 (RDN : Sham) |

### Why This Trial Mattered
- First **sham-controlled** RDN trial
- Prior trials (HTN-1, HTN-2) were unblinded → inflated results
- Industry needed FDA approval → required rigorous design

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| RDN | 364 | Symplicity catheter (Medtronic) — RF ablation |
| Sham | 171 | Renal angiogram only (no ablation) |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Efficacy Endpoint
**Change in office SBP at 6 months**

### Secondary Endpoints
- Change in 24-hour ambulatory BP
- BP response rate (≥10 mmHg reduction)
- Safety (renal function, renovascular complications)

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (6 Months)

| Outcome | RDN | Sham | Difference | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|------------|---------|
| **Office SBP change** | **-14.1 mmHg** | **-11.7 mmHg** | **-2.4 mmHg** | **0.26** |

**Failed to meet primary endpoint — no significant difference**

### Ambulatory BP (24-Hour)

| Outcome | RDN | Sham | Difference | P-value |
|---------|-----|------|------------|---------|
| 24h SBP change | -6.8 mmHg | -4.8 mmHg | -2.0 mmHg | 0.98 (superiority) |

### BP Response Rates

| Definition | RDN | Sham |
|------------|-----|------|
| Office SBP ≥10 mmHg drop | 58% | 49% |
| Office SBP ≥20 mmHg drop | 39% | 32% |

**No significant differences in response rates**

### Safety

| Outcome | RDN | Sham |
|---------|-----|------|
| Major adverse events | 1.4% | 0.6% |
| Renal artery complications | 0.3% | 0% |
| eGFR change | No difference | — |

## Why Did It Fail?

### The Post-Hoc Autopsy Revealed Multiple Issues

#### 1. Incomplete Denervation
| Problem | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Catheter design | Single-electrode, difficult to achieve circumferential ablation |
| Operator inexperience | Many operators doing first cases during trial |
| No ablation verification | No way to confirm nerves were actually ablated |
| Anatomic variability | Nerves not always reached by RF energy |

#### 2. Patient Selection
| Problem | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| True resistant HTN? | Many patients not on optimized meds |
| Adherence issues | Medication non-adherence common |
| Isolated systolic HTN | Some patients may have had ISH (less sympathetic-driven) |
| African American population | 26% of cohort — may have different HTN pathophysiology |

#### 3. Trial Design Issues
| Problem | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Single-blind only | Patients knew they might get sham |
| 6-month endpoint | May be too short |
| Office BP primary | 24h ABPM may be better endpoint |

#### 4. The Sham Effect
- Sham arm dropped 11.7 mmHg — massive placebo effect
- Patients in trials get better care, better adherence
- Hard to beat placebo when intervention is modest

---

## The Aftermath

### Immediate Impact
- **Medtronic paused RDN program** (2014-2017)
- **FDA approval delayed** indefinitely
- **Field declared "dead"** by many
- **Research funding dried up**

### What We Learned
| Lesson | Application to Future Trials |
|--------|------------------------------|
| Need multi-electrode catheters | Circumferential ablation essential |
| Need ablation verification | Measure renal norepinephrine spillover |
| Need better patient selection | Confirm true resistant HTN, adherence |
| Need 24h ABPM as primary | More reliable than office BP |
| Need medication standardization | Either wash-out or witnessed dosing |

## The Resurrection: SPYRAL Trials

### SPYRAL HTN-OFF MED (2017)

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Design | RDN vs sham, OFF all meds |
| 24h SBP reduction | -5.5 mmHg (RDN) vs -0.5 mmHg (sham) |
| P-value | 0.0018 |
| Result | **Positive — proof of concept** |

### SPYRAL HTN-ON MED (2018)

| Parameter | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Design | RDN vs sham, ON 1-3 meds |
| 24h SBP reduction | -9.0 mmHg (RDN) vs -2.4 mmHg (sham) |
| P-value | <0.001 |
| Result | **Positive — confirms efficacy on meds** |

### What Changed?
1. **Multi-electrode catheter** (Spyral design)
2. **Circumferential ablation** (4 quadrants)
3. **Medication wash-out or witnessed adherence**
4. **24h ABPM primary endpoint**
5. **Experienced operators only**

## Clinical Pearls

1. **SYMPLICITY HTN-3 failed because of execution, not concept** — the biology is real
2. **Sham control is essential** — HTN-1/2 were false positives
3. **Multi-electrode circumferential ablation is mandatory** — single-point RF doesn't work
4. **24h ABPM > office BP** — use the right endpoint
5. **The resurrection worked** — SPYRAL and RADIANCE proved RDN efficacy
6. **Patient selection matters** — truly resistant, adherent patients benefit most

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> SYMPLICITY HTN-3 is the most important "negative" trial in interventional cardiology. It didn't kill RDN — it taught us how to do it right. The failures led to better catheters, better patient selection, and better trial design. **RDN works when done properly**, and the SPYRAL/RADIANCE programs proved it.

> For operators building RDN programs today: the lessons of HTN-3 are your curriculum. Multi-electrode ablation, circumferential coverage, verified denervation, and careful patient selection are non-negotiable.
