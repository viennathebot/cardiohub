# CULPRIT-SHOCK

**Culprit Lesion Only PCI versus Multivessel PCI in Cardiogenic Shock**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2017 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1710261 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01927549 |
| **Bottom Line** | Culprit-lesion-only PCI superior to immediate multivessel PCI in AMI with cardiogenic shock |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 706 |
| **Centers** | 83 sites, Germany, Europe |
| **Population** | AMI with cardiogenic shock and multivessel CAD |
| **Mean Age** | 70 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Inclusion
- Acute MI (STEMI or NSTEMI) with planned PCI
- Cardiogenic shock (SBP <90 for >30 min or vasopressors)
- Multivessel CAD (≥70% stenosis in ≥2 vessels)

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Culprit-only | 344 | PCI of infarct artery only; staged PCI later if needed |
| Multivessel | 341 | Immediate PCI of all significant stenoses |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of death or severe renal failure requiring RRT at 30 days**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause mortality at 30 days
- Renal replacement therapy
- Recurrent MI
- Repeat revascularization
- Time to hemodynamic stabilization

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (30 Days)

| Outcome | Culprit-Only | Multivessel | RR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|--------------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Death or RRT** | **45.9%** | **55.4%** | **0.83 (0.71-0.96)** | **0.01** |

**ARR = 9.5%** | **NNT = 11**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | Culprit-Only | Multivessel | RR | P-value |
|---------|--------------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **All-cause death** | **43.3%** | **51.6%** | **0.84** | **0.03** |
| Renal replacement | 11.6% | 16.4% | 0.71 | 0.07 |

### 30-Day Mortality: NNT = 12

| Endpoint | Culprit-Only | Multivessel |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Death at 30 days | 43.3% | 51.6% |
| Absolute difference | -8.3% | — |
| NNT | 12 | — |

---

## Long-Term Follow-Up

### 1-Year Results

| Outcome | Culprit-Only | Multivessel | P-value |
|---------|--------------|-------------|---------|
| All-cause death | 50.0% | 56.9% | NS |
| Death or RRT | 52.0% | 59.5% | NS |
| Recurrent MI | 1.7% | 2.1% | NS |
| Repeat revasc | 32.3% | 9.4% | <0.001 |

**Mortality signal attenuated at 1 year; more repeat revasc in culprit-only**

---

## Why Multivessel PCI Was Harmful

### Proposed Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Contrast nephropathy | More contrast → more AKI |
| Procedure time | Longer cases → hemodynamic instability |
| No-reflow | Additional vessel manipulation |
| Hemorrhage | More access, more bleeding |
| Incomplete revascularization | Non-culprit lesions often non-flow-limiting |

### The Contrast Story

| Parameter | Culprit-Only | Multivessel |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Contrast volume (median) | 190 mL | 250 mL |
| Procedure time | Shorter | Longer |
| AKI rates | Lower | Higher |

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **Guidelines updated** — culprit-only is now standard of care
2. **Previous practice reversed** — complete revasc was common pre-CULPRIT
3. **Staged PCI strategy** — non-culprit lesions addressed after stabilization
4. **Risk-benefit recalibrated** — immediate complete revasc = more harm

### Guideline Recommendations

| Society | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| ESC 2023 STEMI | Culprit-only PCI (Class I) |
| ACC/AHA 2021 | Culprit-only PCI (Class IIa) |
| SCAI 2021 | Culprit-only unless ongoing ischemia |

---

## The COMPLETE vs CULPRIT-SHOCK Paradox

### Opposite Conclusions

| Trial | Population | Result |
|-------|------------|--------|
| **COMPLETE** | Stable STEMI + MVD | **Complete revasc better** |
| **CULPRIT-SHOCK** | Cardiogenic shock + MVD | **Culprit-only better** |

### Resolution

| Factor | COMPLETE | CULPRIT-SHOCK |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Hemodynamic status | Stable | Shock |
| Mortality | Low (~4%) | High (~45%) |
| Complete revasc | Staged (median 3 days) | Immediate |
| Procedural risk | Low | High |

**Key insight:** In STABLE STEMI, staged complete revasc is beneficial. In SHOCK, immediate complete revasc is harmful. The timing and hemodynamic status matter.

---

## When Complete Revasc May Be Needed in Shock

### Potential Exceptions
- Multiple culprits (true multivessel ACS)
- Ongoing ischemia despite culprit PCI
- Hemodynamic support in place (ECMO, Impella)
- Short additional procedure for critical stenosis

### Practical Approach
1. Treat the culprit
2. Stabilize
3. Re-evaluate non-culprit lesions
4. Stage if needed (days to weeks)

---

## Limitations

1. **Open-label** — no blinding possible
2. **Definition of culprit** — sometimes unclear in multivessel disease
3. **No FFR use** — anatomic severity only
4. **Variable shock severity** — heterogeneous population
5. **1-year signal attenuated** — 30-day benefit may not persist

---

## Controversies

### "What About Mechanical Support?"
- CULPRIT-SHOCK: ~30% had mechanical support
- Would Impella/ECMO allow safe complete revasc?
- Ongoing trials (e.g., DanGer Shock) addressing this

### Non-Culprit Lesion Ischemia
- What if non-culprit is causing ongoing ischemia?
- Judgment call — may need to address
- But routine complete revasc = harm

### 1-Year Mortality Convergence
- 30-day benefit clear
- 1-year: 50% vs 57% (NS)
- But more repeat revasc in culprit-only (32% vs 9%)
- Trade-off: fewer deaths vs more procedures

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Culprit-only in shock** — NNT = 12 to save a life
2. **COMPLETE ≠ CULPRIT-SHOCK** — stable vs shock, staged vs immediate
3. **More contrast = more AKI** — minimize contrast in shock
4. **Stage the non-culprits** — address after stabilization
5. **32% repeat revasc** — expected with culprit-only strategy

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> CULPRIT-SHOCK proved that in **AMI with cardiogenic shock**, immediate multivessel PCI is harmful. Treat the culprit, stabilize the patient, then consider staged PCI for non-culprit lesions later. The **NNT of 12** to save a life is compelling. Don't be tempted to "finish the job" when the patient is crashing — less is more in shock.

> **In the cath lab:** "The patient is in shock. We're going to open the culprit and get out. We can stage the other lesions when they're stable. Every extra minute of contrast and procedure time in shock increases mortality."
