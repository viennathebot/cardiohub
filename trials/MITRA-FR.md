# MITRA-FR

**Percutaneous Repair with the MitraClip Device for Severe Functional/Secondary Mitral Regurgitation**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2018 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1805374 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01920698 |
| **Bottom Line** | MitraClip did NOT reduce death or HF hospitalization — the "negative" counterpart to COAPT |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized controlled trial |
| **N** | 304 |
| **Centers** | 37 French sites |
| **Population** | Symptomatic HFrEF with severe secondary MR |
| **Mean Age** | 70 years |
| **Mean LVEF** | 33% |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| MitraClip + GDMT | 152 | Percutaneous repair + medical therapy |
| GDMT alone | 152 | Guideline-directed medical therapy |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of death or unplanned HF hospitalization at 12 months**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause mortality
- HF hospitalization
- Quality of life (MLHFQ)
- NYHA functional class

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint (12 Months)

| Outcome | MitraClip | GDMT | OR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----------|------|-------------|---------|
| **Death or HF hospitalization** | **54.6%** | **51.3%** | **1.16 (0.73-1.84)** | **0.53** |

**No significant difference**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | MitraClip | GDMT | P-value |
|---------|-----------|------|---------|
| All-cause death | 24.3% | 22.4% | NS |
| HF hospitalization | 48.7% | 47.4% | NS |
| CV death | 21.7% | 20.4% | NS |

### MR Reduction

| Grade | MitraClip | GDMT |
|-------|-----------|------|
| MR ≤ 2+ at 12 months | 83.4% | 16.0% |

**MitraClip dramatically reduced MR — but outcomes didn't improve**

---

## Why Did MITRA-FR Fail While COAPT Succeeded?

### The Proportionality Hypothesis

| Parameter | MITRA-FR | COAPT |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Mean EROA** | **31 mm²** | **41 mm²** |
| **Mean LVEDVI** | **135 mL/m²** | **101 mL/m²** |
| **EROA/LVEDVI ratio** | **0.23** | **0.41** |
| **Interpretation** | Proportionate MR | Disproportionate MR |

### The Core Concept

**MITRA-FR patients:** Dilated ventricle causing MR → HF driving MR
- Fix the MR, but the bad ventricle remains
- MR is a marker, not a driver
- Fixing the marker doesn't fix the disease

**COAPT patients:** Severe MR out of proportion to LV dilation → MR driving HF
- MR is the primary problem
- Fix the MR, patient improves
- MR is a driver, not just a marker

---

## Key Differences: MITRA-FR vs COAPT

| Factor | MITRA-FR | COAPT |
|--------|----------|-------|
| N | 304 | 614 |
| EROA (mean) | 31 mm² | 41 mm² |
| LVEDVI (mean) | 135 mL/m² | 101 mL/m² |
| LVEF (mean) | 33% | 31% |
| MR severity | Moderate-severe | Severe |
| LV dilation | More dilated | Less dilated |
| EROA/LVEDVI | 0.23 | 0.41 |
| Primary endpoint | Death or HF hosp at 1yr | HF hosp at 2yr |
| Result | **Negative** | **Positive** |

---

## Clinical Impact

### What We Learned
1. **Patient selection is everything** — not all secondary MR benefits from repair
2. **Proportionality matters** — the EROA/LVEDVI concept emerged
3. **Guidelines refined** — now specify COAPT-like criteria
4. **Don't clip dilated ventricles** — LVEDVI >96 mL/m² predicts futility

### The Published Same Day Drama
- MITRA-FR and COAPT published simultaneously in NEJM (Sept 2018)
- Opposite results created confusion
- Took months to reconcile findings
- Proportionality hypothesis emerged as explanation

---

## Current Selection Criteria (Post MITRA-FR/COAPT)

### Favor MitraClip (COAPT-like)
- [ ] EROA ≥ 30 mm² (severe MR)
- [ ] LVEDVI ≤ 96 mL/m² (not too dilated)
- [ ] LVEF 20-50%
- [ ] Symptomatic despite GDMT
- [ ] EROA/LVEDVI ratio > 0.4 (disproportionate)

### Avoid MitraClip (MITRA-FR-like)
- [ ] LVEDVI > 96 mL/m² (too dilated)
- [ ] EROA < 30 mm² (MR not severe enough)
- [ ] EROA/LVEDVI ratio < 0.2 (proportionate MR)
- [ ] End-stage cardiomyopathy

---

## Limitations

1. **Smaller N than COAPT** — 304 vs 614
2. **Different endpoint** — 1-year vs 2-year primary
3. **Different MR thresholds** — included less severe MR
4. **GDMT optimization** — may have been better than COAPT control
5. **Single country** — French sites only

---

## The Reconciliation

### Initial Confusion
- "MitraClip doesn't work" vs "MitraClip saves lives"
- Both technically correct — for their populations

### Resolution
- COAPT = Severe MR + modest LV dilation → Clip works
- MITRA-FR = Moderate MR + severe LV dilation → Clip doesn't help
- **The ventricle tells you whether to clip**

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **LVEDVI > 96 mL/m² = stop** — dilated ventricle won't benefit
2. **EROA/LVEDVI ratio is your guide** — >0.4 favors clipping
3. **Severe MR required** — EROA ≥30 mm², not just "moderate"
4. **MR reduction ≠ outcome improvement** — MITRA-FR proved this
5. **Both trials are right** — for different patients

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> MITRA-FR is as important as COAPT because it defines **who NOT to clip**. When MR is proportionate to LV dilation (big ventricle, modest MR), the problem is the ventricle — and MitraClip won't fix that. Use the EROA/LVEDVI ratio: >0.4 suggests MR is the driver (clip it); <0.2 suggests HF is the driver (don't clip).

> **The lesson:** Same procedure, different patients, opposite results. Selection is everything.
