# PARADIGM-HF

**Prospective Comparison of ARNI with ACEI to Determine Impact on Global Mortality and Morbidity in Heart Failure**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2014 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1409077 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT01035255 |
| **Bottom Line** | Sacubitril/valsartan superior to enalapril for mortality and HF hospitalization in HFrEF |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, active-controlled |
| **N** | 8,442 |
| **Centers** | 1,043 sites, 47 countries |
| **Population** | HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%), NYHA II-IV, elevated natriuretic peptides |
| **Mean Age** | 64 years |
| **Mean LVEF** | 29% |
| **Median Follow-up** | 27 months |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Entry Criteria
- LVEF ≤40% (changed to ≤35% mid-trial)
- Elevated BNP ≥150 pg/mL or NT-proBNP ≥600 pg/mL
- On stable ACEi/ARB for ≥4 weeks

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Sacubitril/valsartan | 4,187 | 97/103 mg BID (target dose) |
| Enalapril | 4,212 | 10 mg BID (target dose) |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**Composite of CV death or HF hospitalization**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- All-cause mortality
- CV mortality
- HF hospitalization (first occurrence)
- Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire (KCCQ)

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint

| Outcome | Sacubitril/Valsartan | Enalapril | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|----------------------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **CV death or HF hosp** | **21.8%** | **26.5%** | **0.80 (0.73-0.87)** | **<0.001** |

**20% relative risk reduction**

### Component Analysis

| Outcome | Sacubitril/Valsartan | Enalapril | HR | P-value |
|---------|----------------------|-----------|-----|---------|
| CV death | 13.3% | 16.5% | 0.80 | <0.001 |
| HF hospitalization | 12.8% | 15.6% | 0.79 | <0.001 |
| **All-cause death** | **17.0%** | **19.8%** | **0.84** | **<0.001** |

### NNT Calculations

| Outcome | ARR | NNT (27 months) |
|---------|-----|-----------------|
| CV death or HF hosp | 4.7% | 21 |
| All-cause death | 2.8% | 36 |
| CV death | 3.2% | 31 |

### Quality of Life (KCCQ at 8 months)

| Metric | Sacubitril/Valsartan | Enalapril | P-value |
|--------|----------------------|-----------|---------|
| Clinical summary score | -2.99 | -4.63 | 0.001 |

## Safety

| Adverse Event | Sacubitril/Valsartan | Enalapril |
|---------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Hypotension | 14% | 9.2% |
| Cough | 11.3% | 14.3% |
| Hyperkalemia (>6.0) | 4.3% | 5.6% |
| Renal impairment | 3.3% | 4.5% |
| Angioedema | 0.5% | 0.2% |

**More hypotension with ARNI, but less cough and hyperkalemia**

---

## Trial Stopped Early

- **Data Safety Monitoring Board** stopped trial early for overwhelming efficacy
- Originally planned for 8,800 patients
- Stopped at 8,442 with 2,031 primary events

---

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **New drug class** — ARNI (neprilysin inhibitor + ARB) validated
2. **Guideline upgrade** — ARNI became Class I for HFrEF
3. **RAAS replacement** — ARNI preferred over ACEi/ARB in HFrEF
4. **Mortality benefit** — rare for HF drug to reduce all-cause death
5. **Quality of life** — not just survival, patients feel better

### Current Guidelines (2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA)

| Recommendation | Class |
|----------------|-------|
| ARNI preferred over ACEi/ARB in HFrEF | **Class I** |
| For patients tolerating ACEi/ARB, switch to ARNI | **Class I** |
| Can initiate ARNI de novo (without prior ACEi) | Class IIb |

---

## ARNI in Practice

### Starting ARNI
1. Stop ACEi for 36 hours (washout — angioedema risk)
2. Can switch directly from ARB (no washout)
3. Start low: 24/26 mg BID or 49/51 mg BID
4. Titrate to target: 97/103 mg BID over 2-4 weeks

### Key Considerations
- Watch for hypotension (start low if borderline BP)
- Check K+ and creatinine
- Angioedema risk (rare but serious)
- Cost — significantly more expensive than enalapril

## The Four Pillars of HFrEF (Post-PARADIGM Era)

| Class | Example | Mortality Benefit |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **ARNI** | Sacubitril/valsartan | Yes (PARADIGM-HF) |
| **Beta-blocker** | Carvedilol, metoprolol | Yes |
| **MRA** | Spironolactone, eplerenone | Yes (RALES, EMPHASIS-HF) |
| **SGLT2i** | Dapagliflozin, empagliflozin | Yes (DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced) |

**GDMT now means all four classes**

---

## Limitations

1. **Active control only** — no placebo arm
2. **Run-in period** — excluded those intolerant to either drug
3. **Expensive drug** — access issues in some healthcare systems
4. **Population** — mostly NYHA II; less data in NYHA IV
5. **Baseline therapy** — some patients not on optimal background therapy

---

## PARADIGM-HF's Legacy

### Subsequent Trials

| Trial | Population | Result |
|-------|------------|--------|
| PIONEER-HF | Acute HF (in-hospital) | ARNI safe and effective early |
| TRANSITION | Post-discharge HF | In-hospital initiation feasible |
| PARAGON-HF | HFpEF | NS overall, but benefit in women/lower LVEF |

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **NNT = 21 for primary endpoint** — highly effective
2. **Reduces death** — one of few HF drugs with all-cause mortality benefit
3. **36-hour ACEi washout required** — mandatory for angioedema prevention
4. **Hypotension is main barrier** — start low, go slow
5. **Part of "4 pillars"** — combine with BB, MRA, SGLT2i

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> PARADIGM-HF established sacubitril/valsartan as the **preferred neurohormonal agent in HFrEF**, replacing ACEi/ARB as the cornerstone. The **all-cause mortality reduction** (NNT 36) puts it alongside beta-blockers and MRAs as truly life-saving HF therapy. If your HFrEF patient isn't on an ARNI, ask why.
