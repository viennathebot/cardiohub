# TWILIGHT

**Ticagrelor with or without Aspirin in High-Risk Patients after PCI**

---

## Quick Facts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Year** | 2019 |
| **Journal** | NEJM |
| **DOI** | 10.1056/NEJMoa1908419 |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | NCT02270242 |
| **Bottom Line** | Ticagrelor monotherapy after 3 months DAPT reduces bleeding without increasing ischemic events |

---

## Design

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled |
| **N** | 7,119 |
| **Centers** | 187 sites, 11 countries |
| **Population** | High-risk PCI patients completing 3 months DAPT without events |
| **Mean Age** | 65 years |
| **Randomization** | 1:1 |

### Key Design Feature
- **Run-in phase:** All patients received ticagrelor + aspirin for 3 months
- **Randomization at 3 months:** Only event-free patients randomized
- **High-risk definition:** Clinical or angiographic features

### High-Risk Criteria (≥1 required)
- Age ≥65
- Female requiring PCI
- Troponin-positive ACS
- Diabetes mellitus
- CKD (eGFR <60)
- Multivessel CAD
- Prior PCI or MI
- Complex PCI (bifurcation, CTO, ≥3 stents, etc.)

### Arms

| Arm | N | Intervention |
|-----|---|--------------|
| Ticagrelor monotherapy | 3,555 | Ticagrelor 90mg BID + placebo |
| DAPT | 3,564 | Ticagrelor 90mg BID + ASA 81-100mg |

---

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint
**BARC 2, 3, or 5 bleeding at 1 year (from randomization)**

### Key Secondary Endpoints
- Death, MI, or stroke (ischemic composite)
- Stent thrombosis
- All-cause death
- BARC 3 or 5 bleeding (major bleeding)

---

## Results

### Primary Endpoint: Bleeding

| Outcome | Ticagrelor Mono | DAPT | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----------------|------|-------------|---------|
| **BARC 2/3/5 bleeding** | **4.0%** | **7.1%** | **0.56 (0.45-0.68)** | **<0.001** |

**ARR = 3.1%** | **NNT = 32** (to prevent one bleed)

### Bleeding by Severity

| BARC Type | Ticagrelor Mono | DAPT | HR |
|-----------|-----------------|------|-----|
| BARC 2 | 2.8% | 4.7% | 0.60 |
| BARC 3 | 1.1% | 2.2% | 0.49 |
| BARC 5 (fatal) | 0.1% | 0.1% | NS |

### Ischemic Outcomes (Safety)

| Outcome | Ticagrelor Mono | DAPT | HR (95% CI) | P-value |
|---------|-----------------|------|-------------|---------|
| **Death, MI, or stroke** | **3.9%** | **3.9%** | **0.99 (0.78-1.25)** | **NS** |
| MI | 2.6% | 2.5% | NS | — |
| Stroke | 0.5% | 0.6% | NS | — |
| All-cause death | 1.1% | 1.2% | NS | — |
| Definite stent thrombosis | 0.4% | 0.6% | NS | — |

**No increase in ischemic events with aspirin discontinuation**

## Clinical Impact

### What Changed
1. **New DAPT paradigm** — short DAPT + P2Y12 monotherapy validated
2. **Bleeding focus** — shifted conversations from "more is better" to "optimize risk-benefit"
3. **High-risk patient data** — showed safety in complex patients
4. **Guideline updates** — P2Y12 monotherapy after short DAPT now Class IIa

### Practical Application

| Phase | Duration | Regimen |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Initial DAPT | 1-3 months | Ticagrelor + ASA |
| De-escalation | 9-12+ months | Ticagrelor monotherapy |

## Related Trials: The De-escalation Era

| Trial | N | Strategy | Result |
|-------|---|----------|--------|
| **TWILIGHT** | 7,119 | Ticagrelor mono after 3mo | ↓ Bleeding, = Ischemia |
| GLOBAL LEADERS | 15,991 | Ticagrelor mono after 1mo | NS for superiority |
| TICO | 3,056 | Ticagrelor mono after 3mo | ↓ Bleeding, = Ischemia |

| MASTER DAPT | 4,579 | Abbreviated DAPT in HBR | ↓ Bleeding, = Ischemia |

**Consistent signal: shorter DAPT + P2Y12 monotherapy reduces bleeding without ischemic penalty**

---

## Limitations

1. **Enriched population** — only event-free patients at 3 months randomized
2. **Ticagrelor-specific** — may not generalize to clopidogrel or prasugrel
3. **1-year follow-up** — long-term effects unknown
4. **High-risk but selected** — excluded very high bleed risk
5. **Industry-sponsored** — AstraZeneca funded

---

## Controversies

### "Which P2Y12 Inhibitor for Mono?"
- TWILIGHT used ticagrelor
- STOPDAPT-2 used clopidogrel (also worked)
- Clopidogrel is cheaper and once-daily
- No head-to-head mono comparison exists

### Optimal DAPT Duration Before De-escalation
- TWILIGHT: 3 months
- GLOBAL LEADERS: 1 month (didn't reach superiority)
- TICO: 3 months
- **Consensus:** 1-3 months seems safe

### Cost Considerations
- Ticagrelor: ~$400/month
- Clopidogrel: ~$10/month
- If de-escalating, consider clopidogrel mono (STOPDAPT-2 data)

## Current Guidelines (2023-2024)

| Guideline | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| ACC/AHA | P2Y12 monotherapy after 1-3mo DAPT is reasonable (Class IIa) |
| ESC | De-escalation to P2Y12 mono may be considered (Class IIb) |
| Consensus | Individualize based on bleeding vs ischemic risk |

---

## Clinical Pearls

1. **3.1% ARR for bleeding** — clinically meaningful
2. **No ischemic penalty** — stent thrombosis not increased
3. **Works in high-risk patients** — ACS, diabetes, complex PCI all included
4. **3 months DAPT = minimum** — before de-escalation
5. **Consider clopidogrel mono** — cheaper, once-daily, STOPDAPT-2 supportive

---

## De-escalation Decision Framework

### Favor Early De-escalation (1-3 mo → P2Y12 mono)
- High bleeding risk (HAS-BLED ≥3)
- Prior bleeding event
- Triple therapy requirement (AF + PCI)
- Patient preference
- Stable CAD

### Favor Extended DAPT (12+ mo)
- Prior stent thrombosis
- Multiple ACS events
- Diffuse CAD, underexpanded stents
- Low bleeding risk
- High thrombotic risk (DAPT score >2)

---

## Key Takeaway for Practice

> TWILIGHT proved that **aspirin is the bleeding culprit**, not ticagrelor. Dropping aspirin after 3 months of DAPT cuts bleeding by 44% without increasing ischemic events. For high-risk PCI patients who tolerate initial DAPT, **ticagrelor monotherapy is a safe and effective de-escalation strategy**.

> **The paradigm shift:** We've moved from "longer DAPT is safer" to "optimized DAPT is smarter."
